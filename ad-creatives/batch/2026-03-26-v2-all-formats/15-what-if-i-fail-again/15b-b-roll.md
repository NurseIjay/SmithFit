# Concept 15: What If I Fail Again? -- B-Roll
**Funnel Stage:** BOF (Retargeting)
**Format:** B-Roll with IJ Voiceover
**Duration:** 30-35 seconds
**Objective:** Visualize the weight of past failures, then the lightness of a safe bet

---

## Shot List

### SHOT 1 -- Drawer of Abandoned Programs (Hook)
- **What to film:** Close-up of a drawer being opened. Inside: a meal plan printout, a workout guide, a resistance band still in its packaging, maybe a food journal with only the first 3 pages filled in. The drawer of things she started and stopped.
- **Camera angle:** Overhead, looking down into the open drawer
- **Movement:** Drawer slides open slowly
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Warm overhead kitchen/bedroom light
- **Props:** Meal plan printout, workout guide or booklet, resistance band in packaging, food journal with a few pages written

### SHOT 2 -- Phone with Cart Abandoned
- **What to film:** Close-up of a phone screen showing a checkout page with "$27.00" and a "Complete Purchase" button. The thumb hovers but doesn't tap. Hesitation. This is the moment.
- **Camera angle:** Over-the-shoulder tight on phone screen
- **Movement:** Static, thumb hovers
- **Duration:** 2.5 seconds
- **Lighting:** Phone screen glow
- **Props:** Phone with mock checkout page showing $27

### SHOT 3 -- Scale Being Pushed Under a Bathroom Cabinet
- **What to film:** Close-up of a foot pushing a bathroom scale under the vanity cabinet with a gentle kick. She's done looking at it. Not angry -- tired.
- **Camera angle:** Low angle from floor level, watching the scale slide
- **Movement:** Static, scale slides
- **Duration:** 2 seconds
- **Lighting:** Bathroom vanity light
- **Props:** Bathroom scale, bathroom floor, vanity cabinet

### SHOT 4 -- Sitting on Bed, Staring at Phone
- **What to film:** Medium shot of someone sitting on the edge of a bed in the dark, phone in hand, screen glow on face. Not scrolling. Just sitting. Thinking. This is the 11 PM moment where she debates starting something new.
- **Camera angle:** Medium from the side, waist height
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Phone screen glow only. Everything else dark. Intimate.
- **Props:** Bed, phone, dim room

### SHOT 5 -- IJ to Camera (The Bridge)
- **What to film:** IJ speaking to camera. Close, warm, genuine. This is the "I get it" moment where a real person looks her in the eyes and names the fear.
- **Camera angle:** Chest-up, eye level, close (1.5 feet)
- **Movement:** Static (tripod)
- **Duration:** 6 seconds (longest single shot -- the emotional anchor)
- **Lighting:** Warm lamp or window light. Soft. Golden.
- **Props:** Clean casual clothes, warm background

### SHOT 6 -- Community Messages on Phone
- **What to film:** Close-up of a phone screen showing community messages: "I almost didn't start either," "Day 3 and I'm shocked," "I can't believe something this simple is working." Thumb scrolling slowly. The proof that 400 people felt the same doubt.
- **Camera angle:** Over-the-shoulder on phone screen
- **Movement:** Slow scroll
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Natural ambient
- **Props:** Phone with community message screenshots

### SHOT 7 -- Hand Tapping "Complete Purchase"
- **What to film:** Close-up of the same phone from Shot 2. This time, the thumb taps the button. The screen transitions to a confirmation page. She did it. Simple, quiet, decisive.
- **Camera angle:** Over-the-shoulder tight on phone screen
- **Movement:** Static, thumb taps
- **Duration:** 2 seconds
- **Lighting:** Phone screen glow, slightly warmer ambient than Shot 2
- **Props:** Phone with mock checkout -> confirmation page

### SHOT 8 -- Morning Light, Glass of Water with Lemon
- **What to film:** Close-up of a clear glass of water with lemon on a kitchen counter. Morning sunlight catches the glass. Quiet. The first day of something new.
- **Camera angle:** Medium close-up, 45-degree angle
- **Movement:** Slight drift
- **Duration:** 2.5 seconds
- **Lighting:** Golden morning sunlight through window
- **Props:** Clear glass, lemon, clean counter, morning light

### SHOT 9 -- Walking Out into Light (Close)
- **What to film:** Someone walking through a doorway into bright morning light. Not dramatic -- just a person who decided to try. Posture up. Calm.
- **Camera angle:** Medium, slightly low, facing the person
- **Movement:** Static, figure walks toward camera
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Bright warm daylight
- **Props:** Casual clothes, doorway, morning light

## Complete Filming Guide

### Summary
- **Total shots:** 9
- **Estimated filming time:** 40-50 minutes
- **Locations needed:** 2 (home interior, exterior door)
- **Wardrobe:** Casual (IJ for to-camera), scrubs optional

### Props Checklist
- [ ] Drawer with abandoned fitness items (meal plan, workout guide, resistance band, food journal)
- [ ] Phone with mock checkout page ($27)
- [ ] Phone with mock confirmation page
- [ ] Bathroom scale
- [ ] Bed, dim room
- [ ] Phone with community message screenshots
- [ ] Clear glass, lemon, morning light
- [ ] Door to exterior

### Location Checklist
- [ ] Bedroom or kitchen (drawer shot)
- [ ] Anywhere (phone shots -- just need phone and hands)
- [ ] Bathroom (scale shot)
- [ ] Bedroom (sitting in dark)
- [ ] Warm room with good light (IJ to camera)
- [ ] Kitchen window (morning light)
- [ ] Exterior door (walk-out)

### Editing Notes
- **Cut pacing:** Shots 1-4 are slow, heavy, weighted. These are the doubt shots. Shot 5 (IJ to camera) is the longest single shot -- let it breathe. Shots 6-9 gradually lighten.
- **Color grade:** Shots 1-4: cooler, slightly muted. Almost monochromatic. Shot 5: warm, golden (the turn). Shots 6-9: bright, warm, hopeful.
- **The pause at Shot 4:** Two full beats of silence before "your fear makes sense." The viewer sitting in the dark with her own phone is the most relatable image in the batch. Let it hold.
- **Shot 7 (tapping purchase):** This is the catharsis shot. The sound of the tap should be slightly audible. Satisfying. Small moment, big feeling.
- **Captions:** Full hardcoded. Bold white, lower third.
- **Music:** None through Shots 1-4 (silence emphasizes the weight). Soft, warm piano enters at Shot 5. Builds gently through 6-9.
