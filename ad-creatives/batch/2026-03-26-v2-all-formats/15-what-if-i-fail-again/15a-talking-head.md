# Concept 15: What If I Fail Again? -- Talking Head
**Funnel Stage:** BOF (Retargeting)
**Format:** Talking Head (IJ to camera)
**Duration:** 28-35 seconds
**Objective:** Name the deepest unspoken objection -- fear of failure -- honor it, then give her permission to try one more time with zero risk

---

## Script (Word-for-Word)

### HOOK (0-5 sec)
**IJ says:** "You're not afraid of spending $27. You're afraid of starting something else and it not working. I get it."

**On-screen text overlay:** "You're afraid of starting again."
**IJ's energy/expression:** Quiet. Direct. No buildup -- just the truth, delivered gently. "I get it" is said with a slight nod, like he's been on the other side of this exact hesitation. Hold eye contact. Don't rush past this.

---

### VALIDATION (5-12 sec)
**IJ says:** "You've started things before. You've been excited on Day 1 and disappointed by Day 14. You've blamed yourself every time. And now the thought of trying one more thing feels... heavy. Like you can't handle one more thing not working."

**On-screen text overlay:** "One more thing not working."
**IJ's energy/expression:** Empathetic. Slow. He's not listing failures to hurt her -- he's proving he understands the weight she carries. "Heavy" gets a pause before it. "One more thing not working" is almost whispered -- it's the quiet thought she has at 2 AM when she sees an ad like this.

---

### THE DIFFERENCE (12-20 sec)
**IJ says:** "So let me tell you why this is different. Not because I'm going to hype you up. But because the math is on your side. You'll know by Day 3. That's not a sales pitch -- 87% of nurses who try this feel different by Day 3. Energy. Cravings calming down. Something shifting. Three days. And if it doesn't happen -- you get your money back. All of it. 60 days to decide."

**On-screen text overlay:** "You'll know by Day 3." (appears at 14 sec)
**IJ's energy/expression:** Shift from soft validation to calm confidence. "Not because I'm going to hype you up" -- he means it. No hype energy. Just facts delivered steadily. "Three days" gets emphasis. "All of it" on the money-back is clear, no fine print energy. He's making it safe.

---

### PERMISSION (20-28 sec)
**IJ says:** "400 nurses felt exactly what you're feeling right now. The same doubt. The same fear. And they tried it anyway. Because the worst that happens is you get your money back. And the best that happens? Everything changes."

**On-screen text overlay:** "The worst that happens: money back. The best: everything changes."
**IJ's energy/expression:** Warm, steady. "400 nurses felt exactly what you're feeling" -- this normalizes her fear. She's not alone in it. "Everything changes" is delivered with genuine belief but not oversell. He's not screaming it. He's saying it like someone who's watched it happen 400 times.

---

### CTA (28-35 sec)
**IJ says:** "The Shift-Proof Metabolism Reset. $27. Built by a nurse. You've already done the hard part -- you've been thinking about it. Now just see for yourself. Link in bio."

**On-screen text overlay:** "$27 | 60-day guarantee | Link in bio"
**IJ's energy/expression:** The lightest energy in the ad. Almost relieved. "You've already done the hard part" reframes the hesitation as progress. "Just see for yourself" is an invitation, not a push. Brief, genuine smile.

## Production Notes

### Setting
- **Location:** Quiet, intimate room. Living room or home office.
- **Background:** Warm, simple. Slightly lived-in. This is a vulnerable conversation.
- **Vibe:** Two people at a kitchen table. One of them is saying the thing the other has been thinking.

### Wardrobe
- **Option 1 (preferred):** Clean casual. T-shirt or hoodie. This isn't about the credential -- it's about human connection. The RN identity comes through in the script.
- **Option 2:** Scrubs with a casual layer.

### Camera
- **Device:** iPhone on tripod
- **Framing:** Chest-up, centered, vertical (9:16 / 1080x1920)
- **Angle:** Eye level. Close -- 1.5-2 feet. Intimate.

### Lighting
- **Primary:** Warm lamp or window light. Soft, golden.
- **Avoid:** Anything bright or clinical. This ad is soft.

### Audio
- **Mic:** Lapel mic. This ad has quiet moments -- audio must be clean.
- **Environment:** Dead quiet. The pauses are part of the delivery.

### Editing
- **Captions:** Hardcoded, bold white with dark shadow. Full transcript.
- **Text overlays:** Fade in gently. Hold longer than standard. "You'll know by Day 3" stays on screen 4 seconds.
- **Cuts:** One maximum. This should feel like an unbroken conversation. The authenticity of continuous eye contact matters here.
- **Color grade:** Warm, soft, golden. Low contrast. Gentle. Second-warmest in the batch after Concept 11.
- **Music:** Very soft piano or acoustic. Barely there. Starts at the validation section, not the hook. The hook should be silent except for his voice.
- **Pacing:** Slow-medium. There are intentional pauses throughout. Do not tighten them in editing. The pauses are where she feels things.
- **End frame:** CTA overlay holds 4 seconds.
