# Ad Batch Psychology Audit Report
**Date:** 2026-03-26
**Batch:** v2-all-formats (40 ads, 10 concepts x 4 formats)
**Auditor Role:** Senior Direct-Response Copywriter & Consumer Psychologist

## OVERALL SCORE: 7.4 / 10

This is a strong first batch -- significantly better than the average ad creative produced for health coaching brands. The cortisol mechanism is well-deployed, IJ's credential is leveraged consistently, and the brand voice is warm without being soft. But there are specific, fixable gaps that would elevate this batch from "good" to "high-converting." The biggest issue: the ads over-index on mechanism education and under-index on the raw emotional life of the avatar. Sarah's inner world -- her shame, her daily rituals, her specific physical experiences -- is referenced but not fully inhabited.

---

## Executive Summary

- **Strongest elements:** Cortisol mechanism is clearly explained across multiple concepts (02, 03, 06). IJ's RN credential is leveraged in every ad. The $27 price transparency and 60-day guarantee are used correctly. Concept 10 ("Feel Like Yourself") is the single best ad in the batch -- it speaks to identity, not just mechanism.
- **Biggest gap #1:** Several avatar quotes from CLAUDE.md are never used verbatim in any ad. "My body just doesn't respond like it used to," "I'm too tired to meal prep," and "I need something that works with night shift" are left on the table. These are the words she literally says to herself -- using them creates instant recognition.
- **Biggest gap #2:** The ads rarely speak to her fear that she is the exception -- that everyone else can lose weight but her. This is the deepest layer of hopelessness, and only Concept 05 (Sarah's story) indirectly addresses it through proof-by-example.
- **Biggest gap #3:** The caregiver identity is almost entirely missing. She takes care of everyone -- patients, kids, family -- and has nothing left for herself. This is the emotional core of her exhaustion, and only one ad (05) references kids at all.
- **Biggest gap #4:** Unwritten objections (cost, fear of failure, time, skepticism about Instagram offers, family judgment) are almost entirely unaddressed. The batch handles the CLAUDE.md objections well but ignores the objections she has but does not say out loud.

### What's Working
The batch uses several avatar quotes directly or nearly verbatim:

| Avatar Quote | Used? | Where? |
|---|---|---|
| "I've tried everything" | YES | 01 ("I'd tried everything"), 02 ("keto didn't work, IF didn't work"), 07 ("But I've tried everything") |
| "I'm good all week then blow it on night shifts" | PARTIAL | 03 references the concept (diets failing after night shift) but never uses the actual quote |

| "I don't even recognize my body anymore" | PARTIAL | 01 caption uses "didn't recognize the person in the mirror" -- close but not her exact words |
| "I need something that works with night shift" | NO | The concept is addressed (03 is entirely about this) but her exact phrasing is never used |

### Gaps Found
- **"My body just doesn't respond like it used to"** -- This is one of the most powerful avatar quotes because it captures the bewilderment of aging + shift work + hormones. It is not used anywhere. It should appear as a hook or opening line in at least one ad.
- **"I need something that works with night shift"** -- This is a search-intent phrase. Women are literally typing this into Google and Instagram. Using it verbatim as a hook would function as both copy AND targeting signal.
- **"I'm just exhausted"** -- The second half of "I'm not lazy" is where the real pain lives. Concept 02 drops it. The exhaustion is the daily experience; the "not lazy" part is the defense. Both matter.

### Specific Fixes Needed
1. **Concept 03 (Night Shift) -- add her exact language to the B hook variation:** Change Variation B to: "I need something that works with night shift. If you've ever said that -- this was built for you." This uses her exact words as the hook.
2. **Create a new hook variation for Concept 02:** Add Variation C: "My body just doesn't respond like it used to. Sound familiar? Here's why -- and it's not your fault."
3. **Concept 04 (Coffee and Vibes):** In the problem section, after "your scrubs are tight, your rings are tight," add: "and your body just doesn't respond like it used to." This stacks three visceral details with one avatar quote.
4. **Concept 07 (Objection Buster):** Change Objection 3 from "But I've tried everything" to "But I've tried everything and my body just doesn't respond anymore." The addition makes it more specific and more painful.

## Dimension 3: Deepest Fears [6 / 10]

### What's Working

| Fear | Addressed? | Where? |
|---|---|---|
| Her body is broken and nothing will work | YES | 02 ("You're not broken"), 05 (Sarah thought her body was broken), 07 (menopause objection) |
| She's the exception -- everyone else loses weight | PARTIAL | 05 implies through "if Sarah can do it, you can too" -- but never names the fear directly |
| She'll always be exhausted | PARTIAL | Exhaustion is named as a problem but the fear of permanence is not addressed |
| She's failing as a nurse (teaches health, can't manage own) | NO | Never addressed in any ad |
| Her body will keep getting worse | NO | Never addressed |
| She'll never feel like herself again | PARTIAL | 10 asks "what if you could feel like yourself again?" -- but the fear is the flip side: what if she NEVER does |
| People notice her weight gain (scrubs, rings) | YES | 04 ("scrubs are tight, rings are tight"), 10 ("not dreading the mirror") |

### Gaps Found
- **"She's failing as a nurse"** -- This is the deepest shame in the batch's target audience and it is completely absent from every ad. She is a healthcare professional who cannot manage her own health. She tells patients to eat well and exercise. She knows the science. And she still cannot do it for herself. This cognitive dissonance is agonizing. Not one ad touches it.
- **"Her body will keep getting worse"** -- The trajectory fear. It is not just that things are bad now -- it is that the trend line is going in the wrong direction. Every month the scrubs are tighter. Every year the energy is lower. No ad names this downward spiral.
- **"She's the exception"** -- The isolation of believing she is uniquely unable to lose weight. The 400+ nurses proof point partially addresses this, but no ad directly says: "You're not the exception. You're not the one person this won't work for."
- **Fear of permanence** -- "I'll always be exhausted" and "I'll never feel like myself again" are existential fears. The ads offer hope but do not first name the fear. Naming the fear before offering hope is what creates the emotional relief that drives action.

### Specific Fixes Needed
1. **Concept 02 (You're Not Lazy) -- add the nurse shame angle:** After "You're not lazy. You're not broken," add: "And the fact that you're a nurse who can't seem to figure out her own health? That's not a character flaw. That's cortisol." This names the shame directly and externalizes it.
2. **Concept 01 (Ghost in Scrubs) -- add trajectory fear:** In the problem section, after "nothing stuck," add: "And every month it was getting worse. Tighter scrubs. Less energy. I could see where this was heading." This names the downward spiral.
3. **Concept 10 (Feel Like Yourself) -- name the fear before the hope:** Open with: "The scariest thought isn't 'I need to lose weight.' It's 'what if nothing ever works for me?'" Then transition to: "What if you could feel like yourself again?" The fear-then-hope sequence is more powerful than hope alone.
4. **Add explicit "you're not the exception" language to at least 2 ads:** In Concepts 05 and 09, add a line like: "You're not the exception. You're not the one nurse this won't work for. 400 nurses thought the same thing."

## Dimension 4: Objections [6.5 / 10]

### What's Working

| Objection | Addressed? | Where? |
|---|---|---|
| "Works on nights?" | YES | 03 (entire concept), 07 (Objection 1) |
| "Do I track macros?" | NO | Never addressed |
| "What if I miss breaks?" | NO | Never addressed |

| "Too exhausted to work out." | NO | Never addressed directly |
| "Is it just a meal plan?" | NO | Never addressed |
| "I can't afford another thing" | PARTIAL | 09 (DoorDash reframe) addresses price but not the "another thing" fatigue |
| "What if I start and fail again?" | NO | Never addressed |
| "I don't have time for this" | PARTIAL | Implied by "7 days" and "2 minutes" (quiz) but never named |
| "Is this just another Instagram scam?" | NO | Never addressed |
| "My husband/family will think it's another waste of money" | NO | Never addressed |

### Gaps Found
The batch handles the 4 objections in Concept 07 well. But 8 of the 12 mapped objections are either missing or only partially addressed. The unwritten objections are particularly dangerous because the avatar will not voice them in comments -- she will simply scroll past.

- **"Do I track macros?"** -- This is one of the 7 CLAUDE.md objections and it appears in zero ads. The reframe ("Structure beats tracking. We stabilize first.") is strong and should be in Concept 07.
- **"What if I miss breaks?"** -- Another CLAUDE.md objection, zero appearances. The reframe ("Built for missed breaks. That's why it works.") is perfect for the rapid-fire format.
- **"Too exhausted to work out"** -- The reframe ("We're not chasing sweat. We're sending a signal.") is one of the strongest lines in the entire CLAUDE.md and it appears nowhere.
- **"Is it just a meal plan?"** -- The reframe ("It's a shift-proof physiology system with templates.") would differentiate from every competitor.
- **"What if I start and fail again?"** -- This is the fear-of-failure objection. It is the reason she hesitates even at $27. The 60-day guarantee partially addresses it, but the emotional objection ("I don't want to feel like a failure again") needs direct acknowledgment.
- **"Is this another Instagram scam?"** -- She has seen 100 fitness coaches in her feed. IJ's RN credential helps, but no ad directly says: "I know you've seen a thousand of these. Here's why this one is different."

### Specific Fixes Needed
1. **Concept 07 (Objection Buster) -- expand to 6 objections or create a Part 2:** Add: "But do I have to track macros?" / "No. Structure beats tracking. We stabilize first." AND "But I'm too exhausted to work out." / "We're not chasing sweat. We're sending a signal to your nervous system." These are among the strongest reframes in CLAUDE.md and they are currently unused.
2. **Create a new retargeting concept: "I Know What You're Thinking"** -- Directly address the skepticism objection: "You've seen a hundred fitness coaches on Instagram. You're wondering if this is just another one. Fair. Here's the difference: I'm an RN. I work shifts. I built this because nothing else worked for me. And 400 nurses have tried it with a 60-day money-back guarantee. If it doesn't work, you get your money back. The only risk is scrolling past this and doing nothing."
3. **Concept 09 (DoorDash) -- add the "another waste" reframe:** After "It's $27," add: "And if it doesn't work, you get your money back. So even if someone at home thinks it's another waste of money -- you're covered." This names the family judgment objection and neutralizes it.
4. **Concept 01 (Ghost in Scrubs) or 10 (Feel Like Yourself) -- add failure-fear acknowledgment:** "I know you're tired of starting things that don't work. I was too. That's why there's a 60-day guarantee -- if you don't feel different, you get your money back. No guilt."

### Gaps Found
- **Caregiver identity completely absent.** She spends 12 hours taking care of patients, comes home and takes care of kids, takes care of aging parents. She has nothing left for herself. This is the emotional engine of her exhaustion and it does not appear in a single ad. "You take care of everyone else. Who's taking care of you?" is a devastating hook that is completely missing.
- **Break room culture never referenced.** The vending machine. The pizza someone brought in. The birthday cake in the break room. These are the specific, daily temptation moments that make her feel like a failure. No ad names them.
- **Charting at 2 AM never referenced.** This is one of the most visceral night-shift moments -- sitting at the nurses' station at 2 AM, staring at a screen, reaching for something to eat because she is exhausted. It is a "you had to be there" detail that signals insider knowledge.
- **Comparing herself to other nurses.** She sees other nurses who seem to have it together -- who meal prep, who go to the gym, who look good in their scrubs. She wonders what is wrong with her. This comparison shame is a major unaddressed trigger.
- **"Putting everyone else first, nothing left for herself"** is the emotional foundation of the avatar and it appears in zero ads.

### Specific Fixes Needed
1. **Create a new concept: "Who Takes Care of You?"** -- Hook: "You spend 12 hours taking care of patients. Then you come home and take care of your kids. Your family. Everyone gets the best of you. But who takes care of you?" Mechanism: "Your body has been in fight-or-flight for years because you never get to rest." CTA: "The Shift-Proof Reset is the first thing that's just for you. $27."
2. **Concept 04 (Coffee and Vibes) -- add break room detail:** After "living off coffee and vibes," add: "...and the pizza someone brought to the break room is the only thing you've eaten since 7 PM." This is hyper-specific and will stop the scroll for any nurse who has lived this exact moment.
3. **Concept 01 (Ghost in Scrubs) B-roll version -- add charting-at-2-AM shot:** The B-roll filming guide should include a shot of someone at a desk in dim light, staring at a screen, with a snack wrapper nearby. This is the night-shift experience in one image.
4. **Concept 03 (Night Shift) -- add the comparison shame:** After "every diet you've tried was designed for people who sleep at night," add: "Meanwhile, you watch other nurses who seem to have it figured out, and you wonder what's wrong with you." Then: "Nothing's wrong with you. Their schedule is different. Or their cortisol hasn't caught up with them yet."

Concept 10 does the heavy lifting for identity shift and does it well. The problem is that it is the ONLY ad carrying this weight. Identity language should bleed into at least 3-4 other concepts.

- **"Looks put-together without trying"** -- See Dimension 2 notes. This is the effortless confidence she craves.
- **"Takes care of herself like she takes care of patients"** -- This is a powerful reframe: she is an expert caregiver who has never applied that expertise to herself. "What if you treated your own health the way you treat your patients' health?" is a hook-worthy identity reframe.

No ad says: "This isn't a lifestyle overhaul. It's a quiet reset that works in the background while you live your life."

1. **Spread identity language across more concepts.** At minimum, Concepts 01, 04, and 05 should each include one identity-shift line in either the script or the caption. Example for 04: "Imagine walking into shift with energy, scrubs fitting right, not reaching for coffee just to function." Example for 01: "I went from ghost to grounded."
2. **Concept 05 (Sarah's Story) -- expand the identity shift:** Sarah's proof section should include more identity language: "She's not meal prepping for hours. She's not doing anything extreme. She just looks and feels like herself -- and other nurses started asking what changed."
3. **Add "quiet transformation" language to Concept 09 (DoorDash):** After listing the value stack, add: "No crazy protocol. No 5 AM workouts. Just a quiet reset that works with your schedule."

### Gaps Found
- **Urgency (4/10):** The batch lacks any "this can't wait" energy. Every ad feels like a standing offer. No ad says: "Every month you wait, your cortisol compounds. Your body doesn't pause while you think about it." The 60-day guarantee creates safety but urgency is the catalyst that makes her act TODAY instead of bookmarking and forgetting.
- **Belonging (5/10):** "400+ nurses" is social proof, not belonging. Belonging sounds like: "A community of nurses who get it. Who don't judge you for the DoorDash after a 16-hour double. Who celebrate when your scrubs fit different." None of the 10 concepts create community warmth.
- **FOMO (3/10):** The batch is almost entirely FOMO-free. The research framework identifies "others are doing this and it's working" as a key trigger, but no ad says something like: "While you're scrolling, 400 nurses are already on Day 3 feeling different. The question isn't whether this works. It's how long you'll wait."
- **Anger (2/10):** The avatar is frustrated at bad advice -- diets that were never built for her, fitness coaches who don't understand shift work, the wellness industry that treats her like a 9-to-5 office worker. This frustration is barely tapped. Concept 03 gets closest ("every diet was designed for people who sleep at night") but stops short of channeling it into righteous anger.

### Specific Fixes Needed
1. **Add urgency to at least 2 BOF ads (08, 09, or 10).** Example for 09: "Every DoorDash order after shift is $27 that maintains the cycle. One $27 reset could break it. Your body doesn't have a pause button." Example for 10: "You've been telling yourself 'I'll figure it out eventually.' What if eventually is right now?"
2. **Create a belonging-first ad or add belonging to Concept 10.** Add to 10's identification section: "Join 400 nurses who stopped doing this alone. Weekly coaching. A community that gets your schedule. People who celebrate when you text 'my scrubs fit today.'"
3. **Add FOMO to Concept 05 (Sarah's Story) caption:** "Sarah almost didn't start. She almost scrolled past this too. She's 30 lbs lighter now."
4. **Create an anger-forward concept.** Hook: "You've been lied to. Every diet you've tried was built for someone who works 9-to-5, sleeps 8 hours, and has time to meal prep. That's not you. And you've been blaming yourself for a system that was never designed for your life." This channels anger at the fitness industry and redirects it toward SmithFit as the nurse-built alternative.
