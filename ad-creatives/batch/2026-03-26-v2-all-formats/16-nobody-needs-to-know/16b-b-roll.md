# Concept 16: Nobody Needs to Know -- B-Roll
**Funnel Stage:** MOF (Warm)
**Format:** B-Roll with IJ Voiceover
**Duration:** 26-30 seconds
**Objective:** Visualize the quiet, private transformation -- no fanfare, no spectacle, just subtle shifts that other people eventually notice

---

## Shot List

### SHOT 1 -- Phone Alarm Going Off, Finger Dismisses It (Hook)
- **What to film:** Close-up of a phone screen showing an alarm at 6:15 AM. A finger calmly dismisses it. No dramatic wake-up. Just a normal morning. But something about it feels intentional.
- **Camera angle:** Overhead tight on phone and hand
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 2 seconds
- **Lighting:** Soft morning light beginning to filter in
- **Props:** Phone showing 6:15 AM alarm

### SHOT 2 -- Morning Cocktail, No Fanfare
- **What to film:** Hands squeezing a lemon into a glass of water. Adding a pinch of salt. Stirring with a spoon. Simple, quiet, unhurried. No one is watching. This is private.
- **Camera angle:** Medium close-up, 45-degree angle
- **Movement:** Slight handheld drift (organic)
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Golden morning sunlight through kitchen window. Warm.
- **Props:** Clear glass, lemon, salt, spoon, clean counter, morning light

### SHOT 3 -- Putting on Scrubs (They Fit)
- **What to film:** Medium shot of someone pulling on scrub top. A quick, subtle check in the mirror -- not anxious, just noticing. The scrubs fit. A micro-expression of satisfaction. No celebration. Just a private moment.
- **Camera angle:** Over-the-shoulder into bathroom mirror
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Bathroom vanity light, warm
- **Props:** Scrubs (should look like they fit well), bathroom mirror

### SHOT 4 -- Walking Past Break Room
- **What to film:** Shot from inside a break room (or kitchen styled to look like one). Someone walks past the open door without stopping. In the foreground, visible on the table: a pizza box, a bag of chips, vending machine snacks. She walks past. Doesn't even pause.
- **Camera angle:** Medium from inside the room, figure passes in the hallway/doorway
- **Movement:** Static, figure moves through frame
- **Duration:** 2.5 seconds
- **Lighting:** Fluorescent (break room) vs. warmer hallway light
- **Props:** Pizza box, chips, snacks on a table, doorway

### SHOT 5 -- Coworker Double-Take
- **What to film:** Two people passing each other in a hallway. One (in scrubs) pauses, looks back at the other, tilts head slightly -- the "you look different" moment. The other person keeps walking, doesn't notice the look. Subtle. Real.
- **Camera angle:** Medium from down the hallway, capturing both people
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Natural or warm hallway light
- **Props:** Two people in scrubs (or casual), hallway

### SHOT 6 -- Closing Laptop at Night (Not Researching Diets)
- **What to film:** Close-up of hands closing a laptop at a kitchen table. A mug of tea sits nearby. She's going to bed. Not googling "how to lose belly fat." Not scrolling fitness accounts. She's done for the day because the system is running in the background.
- **Camera angle:** Medium close-up, slightly overhead
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 2.5 seconds
- **Lighting:** Warm lamp light. Evening.
- **Props:** Laptop, mug of tea, kitchen table

### SHOT 7 -- IJ to Camera (The Explanation)
- **What to film:** IJ speaking to camera. Casual, warm, slight smile. This is the "here's why it works quietly" moment.
- **Camera angle:** Chest-up, eye level, 2 feet
- **Movement:** Static (tripod)
- **Duration:** 4 seconds
- **Lighting:** Warm natural light
- **Props:** Casual clothes, warm background

### SHOT 8 -- Text Message Notification
- **What to film:** Close-up of a phone screen. A text notification pops up from a contact named "Amy (Work)": "Ok seriously what have you been doing?? You look amazing." The phone sits on a counter. She hasn't replied yet. She's smiling.
- **Camera angle:** Overhead on phone, hand visible at edge of frame
- **Movement:** Static, notification slides in
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Natural ambient
- **Props:** Phone with staged text notification

### SHOT 9 -- Walking with Quiet Confidence (Close)
- **What to film:** Medium shot of someone walking in daylight. Good posture. Slight smile. Not a power walk or a fitness moment. Just someone who figured something out. Quietly.
- **Camera angle:** Medium, straight-on, slightly low
- **Movement:** Figure walks toward camera
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Bright, warm natural light
- **Props:** Casual or scrubs, outdoor or hallway

## Complete Filming Guide

### Summary
- **Total shots:** 9
- **Estimated filming time:** 40-50 minutes
- **Locations needed:** 3 (home interior, bathroom, hallway/outdoor)
- **Wardrobe:** Scrubs + casual

### Props Checklist
- [ ] Phone showing 6:15 AM alarm
- [ ] Clear glass, lemon, salt, spoon
- [ ] Scrubs (well-fitting)
- [ ] Bathroom mirror
- [ ] Pizza box, chips, snacks (break room setup)
- [ ] Second person for hallway scene (or film the walk-past solo)
- [ ] Laptop, mug of tea
- [ ] Phone with staged text message notification
- [ ] Casual clothes for walk shot

### Location Checklist
- [ ] Bedroom (alarm shot)
- [ ] Kitchen (morning cocktail, laptop closing)
- [ ] Bathroom mirror (scrubs fitting)
- [ ] Hallway or doorway (break room pass, coworker double-take)
- [ ] Clean background room (IJ to camera)
- [ ] Outdoor or hallway (confident walk)

### Editing Notes
- **Cut pacing:** Even, steady throughout. No dramatic pace changes. This ad should feel like a montage of quiet normalcy. Each shot is 2-3 seconds. No rushing.
- **Color grade:** Warm throughout. Golden, soft. This is the gentlest visual treatment in the batch. No cool tones anywhere. Everything should feel like morning light, even the evening shots.
- **The text message (Shot 8):** This is the emotional payoff of the entire ad. Let it hold. The notification sliding in should feel satisfying -- the private win she's been wanting.
- **Captions:** Full hardcoded. Bold white, lower third. Slightly smaller than action-oriented ads -- the intimacy extends to the text.
- **Music:** Soft, warm acoustic guitar or light piano. Pleasant, not sentimental. Background-of-a-coffee-shop energy. Consistent throughout -- no dramatic shifts. This ad is one mood: quiet confidence.
