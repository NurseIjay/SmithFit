# Concept 04: Coffee and Vibes -- Talking Head
**Funnel Stage:** TOF (Cold)
**Format:** Talking Head (IJ to camera)
**Duration:** 20-24 seconds
**Objective:** Meet the avatar with her own language, break room detail, identity shift, quick pivot to mechanism and offer

---

## Script (Word-for-Word)

### HOOK (0-3 sec)
**IJ says:** "If you're living off coffee and vibes after a 12-hour shift..."

**On-screen text overlay:** "Living off coffee and vibes?"
**IJ's energy/expression:** Slight knowing smile. Like he's sharing an inside joke. Warm, not mocking. Leans in slightly.

---

### PROBLEM (3-10 sec)
**IJ says:** "...and the pizza someone left in the break room is the only thing you've eaten since 7 PM... your scrubs are tight, your rings are tight, and your body doesn't respond like it used to... and you're blaming yourself for it..."

**On-screen text overlay:** "Scrubs tight. Rings tight. Body won't respond."
**IJ's energy/expression:** The smile fades to empathy. Each detail lands -- break room pizza, scrubs, rings. Deliver them with recognition, not judgment. "Body doesn't respond like it used to" -- slow down. That's her words. Slight pause after "blaming yourself."

---

### VALIDATION + MECHANISM (10-15 sec)
**IJ says:** "Can I be honest with you? It's not you. It's your cortisol. Your stress hormones have been jacked for years from shift work. And I built something for exactly this."

**On-screen text overlay:** "It's not you. It's cortisol."
**IJ's energy/expression:** "Can I be honest with you?" delivered like a friend about to tell you something important. Compassionate authority on "it's your cortisol." Confident on "built something."

---

### IDENTITY SHIFT + CTA (15-22 sec)
**IJ says:** "Imagine walking into shift with energy. Scrubs fitting right. Not reaching for coffee to survive. It's $27. It takes 7 days. And 87% of nurses feel different by Day 3. Link in bio."

**On-screen text overlay:** "$27 | 7 days | 87% feel different by Day 3 | Link in bio"
**IJ's energy/expression:** Paint the picture first -- "walking into shift with energy" -- let her see it. Then the offer. Quick, confident delivery. Each fact lands. Warm close. Brief nod toward camera.

## Production Notes

### Setting
- **Location:** Kitchen counter with a coffee mug visible in frame, or simple home background.
- **Vibe:** Casual, conversational. Like FaceTime with a friend who happens to be a nurse.

### Wardrobe
- Casual clothes: solid color tee (black, gray, navy). NOT scrubs for this one -- the casual vibe matches the humor.
- Optional: scrub pants with a casual top (the "I came off shift and changed my top" look).

### Camera
- iPhone on tripod. Vertical 9:16. Chest-up. Eye level. Close -- 2 feet.

### Lighting
- Natural window light. Warm. Kitchen vibes.

### Audio
- Lapel mic or quiet room with phone mic. Clean audio critical for the short format.

### Editing
- **Captions:** Hardcoded. Bold white, dark shadow. Full transcript.
- **Cuts:** Zero or one. This is a single take if possible -- the authenticity of one continuous delivery is the strength.
- **Color grade:** Warm, slightly golden. Feels like morning light.
- **Music:** None. The conversational tone doesn't need music.
- **Pacing:** This is one of the fastest ads in the batch. Keep it tight. If IJ stumbles, film again -- the format demands clean delivery.
- **End frame:** CTA overlay holds 3 seconds.
