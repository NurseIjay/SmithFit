# Concept 05: Sarah's Story -- B-Roll
**Funnel Stage:** MOF (Warm)
**Format:** B-Roll with IJ Voiceover
**Duration:** 34-40 seconds
**Objective:** Tell Sarah's transformation through nurse-life visuals, quiet transformation, "not the exception"

---

## Shot List

### SHOT 1 -- Morning Chaos (Hook)
- **What to film:** Quick montage feel: a kitchen counter with kids' cereal bowls, a badge lanyard, packed lunches being assembled, a coffee mug being grabbed in rush. Hands moving fast. Controlled chaos.
- **Camera angle:** Overhead bird's eye on the counter
- **Movement:** Static, hands move through frame
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Morning kitchen light, warm but slightly rushed feel
- **Props:** Cereal bowls, badge lanyard, lunch containers, coffee mug, kids' items visible

### SHOT 2 -- Scrubs in Car
- **What to film:** Shot through a car windshield: a figure in scrubs sitting in the driver's seat, forehead resting on the steering wheel for a brief moment. Post-shift exhaustion. Then lifts head.
- **Camera angle:** From passenger seat looking at driver, or from outside through windshield
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Parking lot ambient. Daylight through windshield.
- **Props:** Scrubs, car interior, steering wheel

### SHOT 3 -- Sad Scale Moment
- **What to film:** Close-up of a bathroom scale display showing a number (blurred or partially obscured for compliance). A foot steps off. Camera tilts up to show a hand reaching for a small meal-prep container on the counter -- small portions, restrictive eating implied.
- **Camera angle:** Start low on scale, tilt up to counter
- **Movement:** Slow tilt up
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Bathroom light, slightly harsh
- **Props:** Scale (number blurred), small meal-prep container with minimal food

### SHOT 4 -- Calorie App Close-Up
- **What to film:** Close-up of a phone screen showing a calorie tracking app with "987 calories" logged for the day. A finger taps to close the app.
- **Camera angle:** Over-the-shoulder tight on phone screen
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 2 seconds
- **Lighting:** Phone screen glow
- **Props:** Phone with calorie app mocked up showing ~1000 calories

### SHOT 5 -- The Pivot (Regulation)
- **What to film:** Hands opening a journal or workbook to a page labeled "Day 1." A pen rests beside it. Morning light. Clean counter. The shift from restriction to regulation -- calm, intentional.
- **Camera angle:** 45-degree close-up on the journal
- **Movement:** Slight push-in
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Warm morning sunlight
- **Props:** Journal or printed workbook, pen, clean counter

### SHOT 6 -- Full Plate of Food
- **What to film:** Overhead shot of a full, abundant plate of food -- eggs, avocado, toast, fruit. A real meal. Contrast to the tiny portions earlier. A hand reaches in with a fork.
- **Camera angle:** Overhead bird's eye
- **Movement:** Static, then hand enters
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Bright, warm kitchen light. The food looks inviting.
- **Props:** Full plate of food (eggs, avocado, toast, fruit), fork, clean table/counter

### SHOT 7 -- Walking with Energy
- **What to film:** Medium shot of someone walking briskly down a sidewalk or through a park. Scrubs or casual. Good posture. Energy in the step. Not a run -- a confident walk.
- **Camera angle:** Medium from the side, tracking
- **Movement:** Slow tracking shot following the walk
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Bright daylight, warm
- **Props:** Comfortable clothes or scrubs, outdoor setting

### SHOT 8 -- Coworkers Noticing (NEW -- Quiet Transformation)
- **What to film:** Medium shot from across a break room or hallway: the person in scrubs, and a coworker doing a subtle double-take or leaning in to say something. Not staged or dramatic -- a natural "hey, you look different" moment. Can be implied with body language.
- **Camera angle:** Medium wide from a distance, observational
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 2.5 seconds
- **Lighting:** Natural or fluorescent (institutional)
- **Props:** Scrubs on both people, break room or hallway setting

### SHOT 9 -- Scrubs Fitting
- **What to film:** Medium shot: someone in scrubs looking at themselves in a full-length mirror. A small, satisfied smile. Adjusting the scrub top -- it fits well. Not a dramatic pose -- a quiet, private moment of recognition.
- **Camera angle:** Over-the-shoulder into the mirror
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Bedroom or bathroom, natural light
- **Props:** Scrubs (that fit well), full-length mirror

### SHOT 10 -- Family Moment (Sarah's Life)
- **What to film:** Wide shot: adult figure playing with children in a living room or backyard. Laughing. Energy. This is "I feel like myself again" visualized -- not a fitness result, a life result.
- **Camera angle:** Wide, slightly warm/soft focus
- **Movement:** Static or gentle handheld
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Natural, warm, golden hour if outdoors
- **Props:** Living room or backyard setting, kids (or imply family presence)

### SHOT 11 -- IJ to Camera (Close)
- **What to film:** IJ looking at camera with pride and warmth. This is the "she wasn't the exception -- neither are you" face.
- **Camera angle:** Chest-up, eye level
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 4 seconds
- **Lighting:** Warm, natural
- **Props:** Casual or scrubs

## A/B Variations

### Variation A (Default)
- Opens with morning chaos (Sarah's life context)
- Story arc: chaos -> "almost didn't start" -> struggle -> pivot -> results -> quiet transformation -> family

### Variation B (Result-first hook)
- **Opens with Shot 6 (full plate of food)** -- the counterintuitive result first
- Voiceover: "She lost 30 lbs eating MORE than before. She almost didn't start. Here's Sarah's story."
- On-screen text: "She ate MORE. Lost 30 lbs.* She almost didn't start." at 0:00
- Then Shots 1-4 (backstory), then Shot 5, 7-11
- Tests whether leading with the surprising result hooks better than the human story

## Complete Filming Guide

### Summary
- **Total shots:** 11
- **Estimated filming time:** 50-65 minutes
- **Locations:** 3 (home interior/kitchen, car, outdoor area)

### Props Checklist
- [ ] Cereal bowls, kids' items (for kitchen chaos)
- [ ] Badge lanyard
- [ ] Coffee mug
- [ ] Car + steering wheel access
- [ ] Bathroom scale
- [ ] Small meal-prep container (small portions)
- [ ] Phone with calorie app showing ~1000 cal
- [ ] Journal or workbook + pen
- [ ] Full plate of food (eggs, avocado, toast, fruit)
- [ ] Scrubs (2 sets -- one that's tight-fitting, one that fits well)
- [ ] Full-length mirror
- [ ] Second person in scrubs (for coworker noticing shot)
- [ ] Outdoor/backyard area (family moment)

### Editing Notes
- **Cut pacing:** Shots 1-4: medium pacing, building the "before" feeling (2-3 sec each). Shot 5: transitional beat. Shots 6-10: warmer, more hopeful (3 sec each). Shot 11: held for the close.
- **Color grade:** Shots 1-4: slightly desaturated, cooler. Shot 5: transitional. Shots 6-11: warm, bright, golden.
- **Music:** Quiet, slightly melancholy piano for Shots 1-4. Lifts to warm/hopeful at Shot 5. Gentle crescendo at Shot 10 (family moment). Fades under IJ's voice at Shot 11.
- **Captions:** Full hardcoded transcript. Bold white.
- **Key edit:** The food plate reveal (Shot 6) after the calorie-app close-up (Shot 4) -- this contrast is the visual "aha moment." Make the cut clean and slightly slower on the plate reveal. The coworker noticing shot (Shot 8) should feel candid, not staged.
