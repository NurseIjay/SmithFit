# Concept 09: DoorDash Reframe -- B-Roll
**Funnel Stage:** BOF (Hot)
**Format:** B-Roll with IJ Voiceover
**Duration:** 20-24 seconds
**Objective:** Visual price comparison with family objection, urgency, quiet transformation

---

## Shot List

### SHOT 1 -- DoorDash on Phone (Hook)
- **What to film:** Close-up of a phone screen showing a food delivery app. The total shows "$28.47" or similar. A tired hand holds the phone. Late-night setting.
- **Camera angle:** Over-the-shoulder tight on phone
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 2.5 seconds
- **Lighting:** Phone glow in dim room. Post-shift feel.
- **Props:** Phone with delivery app, dim room

### SHOT 2 -- Takeout Container on Counter
- **What to film:** Overhead shot of a takeout container half eaten. Badge lanyard beside it. Phone showing late hour. The aftermath of a post-shift delivery order.
- **Camera angle:** Overhead bird's eye
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 2 seconds
- **Lighting:** Kitchen light, slightly harsh
- **Props:** Takeout container, badge lanyard, phone, counter

### SHOT 3 -- Price Comparison Visual
- **What to film:** Split-frame concept: Left side shows a takeout bag, right side shows the SmithFit reset (phone showing course page or printed workbook). Both sides have price tags: "$28" on takeout, "$27" on the reset.
- **Camera angle:** Overhead, both items side by side
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Bright, clean
- **Props:** Takeout bag, phone with course page or printed workbook, two handwritten price cards

### SHOT 4 -- Value Stack Items
- **What to film:** Quick montage of items placed on counter one by one:
  - Sub-shot A: Metabolic cocktail (water + lemon) placed down
  - Sub-shot B: Printed "Shift-Proof Nutrition" card placed beside it
  - Sub-shot C: Phone showing breathing timer placed next to those
  - Sub-shot D: Card reading "60-Day Guarantee" placed on top
- **Camera angle:** Overhead, items accumulate
- **Movement:** Static, hands place items
- **Duration:** 4 seconds (1 sec per item)
- **Lighting:** Warm, bright kitchen
- **Props:** Glass with lemon water, printed nutrition card, phone with timer, "60-Day Guarantee" card

### SHOT 5 -- Walking with Energy (Quiet Transformation)
- **What to film:** Medium shot of someone in well-fitting scrubs walking confidently through a doorway into bright light. Not dramatic -- quiet, private confidence.
- **Camera angle:** Medium from the front, slightly low angle
- **Movement:** Static, person walks toward camera
- **Duration:** 2 seconds
- **Lighting:** Bright, warm natural light
- **Props:** Well-fitting scrubs, doorway

### SHOT 6 -- IJ to Camera (Close)
- **What to film:** IJ to camera. Confident, slight smile. "Your body doesn't have a pause button" energy. A brief shrug -- "it's a no-brainer."
- **Camera angle:** Chest-up, eye level
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 4 seconds
- **Lighting:** Warm, natural
- **Props:** Casual clothing

## Complete Filming Guide

### Summary
- **Total shots:** 6 (plus 4 sub-shots in Shot 4)
- **Estimated filming time:** 25-30 minutes
- **Locations:** 1 (home kitchen)

### Props Checklist
- [ ] Phone with food delivery app loaded
- [ ] Takeout container + badge lanyard
- [ ] Takeout bag (for comparison shot)
- [ ] Phone with course page or printed workbook
- [ ] Two handwritten price cards ("$28" and "$27")
- [ ] Glass + lemon + water
- [ ] Printed "Shift-Proof Nutrition" card
- [ ] Phone with breathing timer
- [ ] "60-Day Guarantee" printed card
- [ ] Well-fitting scrubs (for walk shot)
- [ ] Casual clothing for IJ

### Editing Notes
- **Cut pacing:** Snappy. Short, direct conversion piece.
- **Color grade:** Shots 1-2: slightly cool/dim (the "waste" vibe). Shots 3-6: warm, bright (the "value" vibe).
- **Key edit:** Shot 3 (price comparison) is the centerpiece. Both prices clearly visible on mobile.
- **Music:** None or very subtle. Directness is the tone.
