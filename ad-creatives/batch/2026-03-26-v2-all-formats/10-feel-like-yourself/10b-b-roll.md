# Concept 10: Feel Like Yourself Again -- B-Roll
**Funnel Stage:** BOF (Hot)
**Format:** B-Roll with IJ Voiceover
**Duration:** 26-30 seconds
**Objective:** Aspirational identity visuals with fear-before-hope, "put together without trying," belonging

---

## Shot List

### SHOT 1 -- Scrubs in Mirror (The Moment)
- **What to film:** Medium shot of someone putting on scrubs in front of a full-length mirror. They look at themselves. A small, satisfied smile. The scrubs fit. No adjusting, no pulling, no sucking in.
- **Camera angle:** Over-the-shoulder into mirror
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Warm bedroom light. Morning.
- **Props:** Well-fitting scrubs, full-length mirror

### SHOT 2 -- Sliding Ring On
- **What to film:** Extreme close-up of a ring sliding easily onto a finger. No resistance. A simple, quiet moment of "it fits."
- **Camera angle:** Extreme close-up on hand
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 2 seconds
- **Lighting:** Natural, warm
- **Props:** Ring, hand

### SHOT 3 -- Walking Out the Door (Put Together Without Trying)
- **What to film:** Medium shot of someone walking out the front door into sunlight. Scrubs on. Bag on shoulder. Good posture. Confidence in the step. Not looking back. The "put together without trying" visual.
- **Camera angle:** From inside the house, looking out through the open door. Person walks out into bright daylight.
- **Movement:** Static. The person moves; the camera stays.
- **Duration:** 2.5 seconds
- **Lighting:** Indoor to outdoor light transition. Sunlight floods through door.
- **Props:** Scrubs, bag, front door

### SHOT 4 -- Cooking a Real Meal
- **What to film:** Medium shot of someone cooking -- stirring a pan, plating food. Not a sad salad. A real, satisfying meal. Smiling slightly while cooking. At ease.
- **Camera angle:** 45-degree medium from the side
- **Movement:** Slight handheld drift (organic)
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Warm kitchen light. Evening.
- **Props:** Pan, real food (colorful -- peppers, eggs, avocado), plate, kitchen

### SHOT 5 -- Post-Shift Energy
- **What to film:** Someone coming home after a shift (scrubs on) and instead of collapsing, they set their bag down calmly, take a breath, and move naturally into their evening. Not a dramatic pose -- a quiet moment of having energy left.
- **Camera angle:** Medium, from the living room looking at the entryway
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Warm evening
- **Props:** Scrubs, bag, entryway, living room

### SHOT 6 -- Laughing with Family/Friends
- **What to film:** Wide shot of someone sitting at a table or couch with others (family, friends, partner). Laughing naturally. Relaxed posture. Present -- not on their phone, not checked out.
- **Camera angle:** Wide, slightly warm/soft focus
- **Movement:** Static or gentle handheld
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Warm, golden
- **Props:** Living room or dining table, other people

### SHOT 7 -- Quiet Morning Moment
- **What to film:** Close-up of hands wrapped around a warm mug (tea or water with lemon). Early morning light. Stillness. The opposite of chaos.
- **Camera angle:** Close-up on hands and mug
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 2.5 seconds
- **Lighting:** Warm morning light, shallow depth of field
- **Props:** Mug, warm beverage, morning light

### SHOT 8 -- IJ to Camera (Close)
- **What to film:** IJ looking at camera. Warm, genuine. A slight nod. "This is what it feels like. And you're not the exception." energy.
- **Camera angle:** Chest-up, eye level, close
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 4 seconds
- **Lighting:** Warm, natural
- **Props:** Casual clothes

## A/B Variations

### Variation A (Default -- fear before hope)
- Opens with scrubs-in-mirror with fear-before-hope voiceover
- Aspirational sequence with belonging and urgency

### Variation B (Contrast opening)
- Opens with a "before" moment: someone staring at scrubs laid out on bed, hesitating. Dim lighting. Then CUTS to the mirror shot with scrubs fitting well. Bright light.
- Voiceover: "Remember when putting on scrubs felt like a test? What if it didn't?"
- On-screen text: "What if it didn't?"
- Adds emotional contrast without a side-by-side image (Meta compliant)

## Complete Filming Guide

### Summary
- **Total shots:** 8
- **Estimated filming time:** 35-40 minutes
- **Locations:** 2 (home interior, outdoor/entryway)

### Props Checklist
- [ ] Well-fitting scrubs
- [ ] Full-length mirror
- [ ] Ring
- [ ] Bag (work bag)
- [ ] Cooking ingredients (colorful food -- peppers, eggs, avocado)
- [ ] Pan, plate, kitchen setup
- [ ] Mug (tea or warm beverage)
- [ ] Casual clothes for IJ (Shot 8)

### Editing Notes
- **Cut pacing:** SLOW. Most cinematic ad in the batch. 2.5-3 seconds per shot. Let each image register.
- **Color grade:** Warm golden throughout. Soft contrast. Slightly dreamy -- not sharp or punchy. Think "Sunday morning."
- **Captions:** Full hardcoded but smaller, more elegant. Consider serif captions for this ad only.
- **Music:** Warm piano or soft acoustic. 20-25% volume. Gentle crescendo at Shot 6 (laughing), then softens.
- **Key note:** Fear-before-hope opening is new. The first 3 seconds name the fear, then the rest of the ad is pure aspiration.
- **End frame:** Overlay holds 4 seconds. Let the viewer sit with the feeling.
