# Concept 02: You're Not Lazy -- Talking Head
**Funnel Stage:** TOF (Cold)
**Format:** Talking Head (IJ to camera)
**Duration:** 26-30 seconds
**Objective:** Validate the avatar's struggle, name the nurse shame, reveal cortisol mechanism, drive link clicks

---

## Script (Word-for-Word)

### HOOK (0-3 sec)
**IJ says:** "You're not lazy. You're not broken."

**On-screen text overlay:** "You're not lazy."
**IJ's energy/expression:** Firm, direct eye contact. Not angry -- compassionate authority. Slight lean forward. This is the most important line -- deliver it like a verdict being overturned.

---

### NURSE SHAME (3-8 sec)
**IJ says:** "And the fact that you're a nurse who can't seem to figure out her own health? That's not a character flaw. That's what chronically elevated cortisol does to your body. You're not the exception. You're exhausted."

**On-screen text overlay:** "You're a nurse who can't fix her own health?" (at 3 sec) then "That's cortisol. Not you." (at 6 sec)
**IJ's energy/expression:** This is the deepest moment in the ad. Slow down. Look her in the eye. "That's not a character flaw" -- pause. Let it land. The nurse shame is real and no one has named it for her before. "You're exhausted" -- softer, validating.

---

### MECHANISM (8-16 sec)
**IJ says:** "When you work 12-hour shifts -- especially nights -- your body stays in fight-or-flight. And when cortisol stays elevated, your body holds onto belly fat no matter what you eat. That's why keto didn't work. That's why intermittent fasting didn't work. That's why more cardio didn't work. You were trying to fix the symptom. The root cause is cortisol."

**On-screen text overlay:** "Your cortisol is in overdrive" (at 9 sec) then "Keto didn't work. IF didn't work. Cardio didn't work." (scrolling, at 12 sec)
**IJ's energy/expression:** Explanatory, measured pace. Each "that's why" builds. Building conviction, not frustration.

---

### PROOF (16-22 sec)
**IJ says:** "You have to calm your nervous system down first. Then everything else starts working. 87% of nurses who try this feel different by Day 3. You're not the exception."

**On-screen text overlay:** "87% feel different by Day 3"
**IJ's energy/expression:** Relief in voice -- the "good news" turn. Warmer. "You're not the exception" -- direct, firm, final.

---

### CTA (22-28 sec)
**IJ says:** "I'm an RN. I built this for us. Link in bio -- it's $27. 60-day guarantee."

**On-screen text overlay:** "$27 | 60-day guarantee | Link in bio"
**IJ's energy/expression:** Confident, warm close. Brief smile. Holds eye contact. Points subtly downward (toward link).

## Production Notes

### Setting
- **Location:** Home, simple background. OR: break room / locker room feel if accessible.
- **Vibe:** Feels like IJ pulled you aside at the nurses' station to tell you something important.

### Wardrobe
- **Primary:** Scrubs (navy or charcoal gray). Badge visible.
- **Alternate for Variation B/C:** Clean casual (solid dark tee).

### Camera
- **Device:** iPhone on tripod
- **Framing:** Chest-up, centered, vertical (9:16 / 1080x1920)
- **Angle:** Straight-on, eye level
- **Distance:** 2-3 feet. Intimate but not uncomfortably close.

### Lighting
- Natural window light from front or 45 degrees. Warm tones preferred.
- If filming in evening: ring light or desk lamp at eye level, diffused.

### Audio
- Lapel mic preferred. Quiet room. No echo.

### Editing
- **Captions:** Hardcoded, bold white with dark shadow. Full transcript.
- **Text overlays:** As noted -- top-center or mid-screen, 2-3 seconds each
- **Cuts:** 1-2 subtle jump cuts to tighten. No flashy transitions.
- **Color grade:** Warm, natural. Slight contrast boost.
- **Music:** None. Voice only. The silence adds weight to "You're not lazy."
- **Pacing:** Let the hook breathe. Full 1-second pause after "You're not broken" before continuing. Let the nurse shame section breathe -- do not rush it.
