# Concept 02: You're Not Lazy -- B-Roll
**Funnel Stage:** TOF (Cold)
**Format:** B-Roll with IJ Voiceover
**Duration:** 28-32 seconds
**Objective:** Cinematic validation sequence -- show the nurse's daily struggle, name the nurse shame, reveal cortisol mechanism

---

## Shot List

### SHOT 1 -- Alarm Clock (Hook)
- **What to film:** Close-up of a phone alarm going off showing 5:15 AM (or 5:45 PM for night shift). A tired hand reaches in to silence it. Scrub top sleeve visible on the wrist.
- **Camera angle:** Overhead close-up on the nightstand/phone
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 2 seconds
- **Lighting:** Dim bedroom. Phone screen is the primary light source. Blue/cool tone.
- **Props:** Phone with alarm screen, nightstand, scrub sleeve visible

### SHOT 2 -- Scale Step
- **What to film:** Close-up of feet in socks stepping onto a bathroom scale. Camera stays on the feet -- we never see the number. The feet pause, then step off.
- **Camera angle:** Low angle from floor level, shooting across the scale surface
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 2.5 seconds
- **Lighting:** Bathroom light, slightly harsh overhead
- **Props:** Bathroom scale, socks

### SHOT 3 -- Scrubs Drawer
- **What to film:** Hands pulling scrub pants from a drawer, holding them up briefly, then tossing them on the bed with a subtle sigh (implied by the toss gesture).
- **Camera angle:** Medium close-up from the side, waist height
- **Movement:** Static with slight handheld drift
- **Duration:** 2 seconds
- **Lighting:** Bedroom ambient, natural morning light from window
- **Props:** Scrub pants, dresser drawer, bed

### SHOT 4 -- Coffee Pour (Break Room Feel)
- **What to film:** Coffee being poured into a mug. Steam rises. A badge lanyard and stethoscope sit on the counter beside the mug.
- **Camera angle:** Medium close-up, 45-degree angle, focused on the pour
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 2 seconds
- **Lighting:** Warm kitchen or break-room lighting. Fluorescent acceptable for authenticity.
- **Props:** Coffee pot/French press, mug, badge lanyard, stethoscope

### SHOT 5 -- Salad Being Eaten (Effort)
- **What to film:** Overhead shot of a healthy salad in a meal-prep container being eaten with a fork. Half-eaten. Phone beside it showing a calorie tracking app (screen mocked up or real).
- **Camera angle:** Overhead bird's eye
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 2 seconds
- **Lighting:** Bright, cafeteria-style or kitchen
- **Props:** Meal prep container with salad, fork, phone with calorie app visible

### SHOT 6 -- Running Shoes by Door (Effort)
- **What to film:** A pair of running shoes sitting by a front door, unworn. Scrub bag/tote sits next to them. The shoes are clean -- untouched today.
- **Camera angle:** Low angle from floor, eye-level with the shoes
- **Movement:** Slight push-in (1-inch zoom)
- **Duration:** 2 seconds
- **Lighting:** Entryway ambient light
- **Props:** Running shoes, scrub bag or tote, front door visible

### SHOT 7 -- Cortisol Visualization (Mechanism)
- **What to film:** Simple whiteboard or notepad where IJ's hand draws a quick stress curve -- a line going up labeled "cortisol" that stays flat/elevated (never comes down). Or: a sticky note that reads "12-hr shift = 12-hr cortisol" stuck on a mirror.
- **Camera angle:** Close-up overhead on the hand drawing / writing
- **Movement:** Following the hand as it writes
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Warm desk lamp
- **Props:** Whiteboard + dry erase marker OR notepad + pen OR mirror + sticky note

### SHOT 8 -- The Reset Moment
- **What to film:** Close-up of IJ's hand placing a glass of water with lemon on a clean counter. Beside it: a small journal or the SmithFit app open on a phone. Morning sunlight. Intentional, calm movement.
- **Camera angle:** 45-degree close-up on the glass and counter
- **Movement:** Slight push-in
- **Duration:** 2.5 seconds
- **Lighting:** Warm morning sunlight -- golden
- **Props:** Glass of water with lemon, small journal or phone with app, clean counter

### SHOT 9 -- IJ to Camera (Close)
- **What to film:** IJ looking directly at camera. Warm, confident, slight nod. Holds eye contact. This is the "I see you and I've been there" moment.
- **Camera angle:** Chest-up, straight-on, eye level
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Natural, warm
- **Props:** Scrubs or casual. Clean background.

## A/B Variations

### Variation A (Default -- above)
- **Opening shots:** Alarm clock -> scale -> scrubs drawer (the daily routine struggle)
- **Opening voiceover:** "You're not lazy. You're not broken. You're exhausted."

### Variation B (Start with the effort)
- **Opening shots:** Shot 5 (salad) -> Shot 6 (shoes) -> Shot 2 (scale) -- start with the effort, then the disappointment
- **Opening voiceover:** "You eat clean. You try to exercise. And your body still won't respond. It's not you. It's your cortisol."
- **On-screen text:** "It's not you." at 0:00
- Remaining shots follow in order. Ends the same.

## Complete Filming Guide

### Summary
- **Total shots:** 9
- **Estimated filming time:** 30-40 minutes
- **Locations needed:** 2 (home interior, simple background for Shot 9)
- **Wardrobe:** Scrubs visible in shots 1, 3, 4; casual OK for Shot 9

### Props Checklist
- [ ] Phone with alarm set to 5:15 AM
- [ ] Bathroom scale
- [ ] Socks
- [ ] Scrub pants (for drawer pull)
- [ ] Dresser drawer
- [ ] Coffee pot or French press + mug
- [ ] Badge lanyard + stethoscope
- [ ] Meal prep container with salad + fork
- [ ] Phone with calorie app screen (mocked up)
- [ ] Running shoes (clean pair)
- [ ] Scrub bag or tote
- [ ] Whiteboard + marker OR notepad + pen OR mirror + sticky note
- [ ] Glass of water + lemon wedge
- [ ] Small journal or phone with SmithFit app

### Location Checklist
- [ ] Bedroom (nightstand, dresser)
- [ ] Bathroom (scale, mirror)
- [ ] Kitchen (counter, coffee, morning light window)
- [ ] Entryway (front door, shoe area)
- [ ] Desk area (for whiteboard/notepad shot)
- [ ] Clean background (for IJ to camera)

### Editing Notes
- **Cut pacing:** Quick cuts (2 sec each) for Shots 1-6 -- builds tension and momentum. Slower cuts (3 sec) for Shots 7-9 as the message shifts to hope.
- **Color grade:** Shots 1-6: slightly desaturated, cool undertone (the struggle). Shots 7-9: warmer, brighter (the solution).
- **Captions:** Full hardcoded captions. Bold white, lower third.
- **Music:** Minimal. Soft, slightly tense ambient for Shots 1-6. Warmer/resolved tone for Shots 7-9. Never louder than voiceover.
- **Key edit:** The transition from Shot 6 to Shot 7 should feel like a shift -- a brief beat of silence, then the music changes. This is the "pivot" moment.
