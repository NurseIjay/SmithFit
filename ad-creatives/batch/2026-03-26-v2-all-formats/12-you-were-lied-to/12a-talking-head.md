# Concept 12: You Were Lied To -- Talking Head
**Funnel Stage:** TOF (Cold)
**Format:** Talking Head (IJ to camera)
**Duration:** 25-32 seconds
**Objective:** Channel frustration at bad advice into righteous anger, then redirect to hope and the offer

---

## Script (Word-for-Word)

### HOOK (0-5 sec)
**IJ says:** "Every piece of diet advice you've ever gotten was designed for someone who works 9-to-5, sleeps 8 hours, and has weekends off. You were set up to fail."

**On-screen text overlay:** "You were set up to fail."
**IJ's energy/expression:** Direct. Not angry -- clear-eyed. Like he's delivering a verdict. Controlled intensity. Eye contact locked. Slight lean forward. The first sentence is fast. "You were set up to fail" lands slower, with weight.

---

### PROBLEM (5-12 sec)
**IJ says:** "And then they blamed you. 'Eat less. Move more. Be more consistent.' Like you're not already running 12-hour shifts, skipping meals because there's no time, and holding it together for everyone around you."

**On-screen text overlay:** "They blamed YOU."
**IJ's energy/expression:** The frustration builds here but it's controlled. He's angry on her behalf, not at her. "Like you're not already running 12-hour shifts" -- he says this with a knowing head shake. He's been there. The list of what she's already doing should feel like evidence in her defense.

---

### MECHANISM (12-20 sec)
**IJ says:** "Here's the truth. Your body doesn't respond to diets the same way when your cortisol has been jacked from shift work for years. Your stress hormones change the rules. The blueprint everyone gave you was never built for your life. You were following the wrong one."

**On-screen text overlay:** "The blueprint was wrong. Not you." (appears at 16 sec)
**IJ's energy/expression:** Shift from frustration to revelation. Calmer. "Your stress hormones change the rules" is delivered with the authority of someone who studied this. "You were following the wrong one" -- lean in, slow down. This is the relief moment.

---

### BRIDGE (20-26 sec)
**IJ says:** "I'm a nurse. I lived your schedule. And I built the first program designed by someone who actually understands shift work. Not a 9-to-5 coach. Not a celebrity trainer. A nurse."

**On-screen text overlay:** "Built by a nurse. For nurses."
**IJ's energy/expression:** Pride without arrogance. "A nurse" at the end is simple, stated. He's not selling a credential -- he's claiming his people. Brief pause after "a nurse." Let it land.

---

### CTA (26-32 sec)
**IJ says:** "The Shift-Proof Metabolism Reset. $27. 60-day guarantee. You've been fighting the wrong fight long enough. Link in bio."

**On-screen text overlay:** "$27 | 60-day guarantee | Link in bio"
**IJ's energy/expression:** The anger has resolved into confidence. "You've been fighting the wrong fight long enough" is delivered with warmth -- it's an invitation to stop fighting, not a command. Slight nod. Brief smile at the close.

## Production Notes

### Setting
- **Location:** Home office or simple room. Could also work in a kitchen or living room.
- **Background:** Clean, neutral. Nothing distracting. The words carry this one.
- **Vibe:** Like a coworker who just found out something that changes everything and pulled you aside to tell you.

### Wardrobe
- **Option 1 (preferred):** Scrubs. The credential is visual. He's one of them.
- **Option 2:** Casual solid tee. Still works -- the script establishes the RN identity verbally.

### Camera
- **Device:** iPhone on tripod
- **Framing:** Chest-up, centered, vertical (9:16 / 1080x1920)
- **Angle:** Eye level. Straight on. Standard distance -- 2-3 feet.

### Lighting
- **Primary:** Natural window light from front or 45 degrees
- **Style:** Clean, well-lit. This isn't a moody ad -- it's a clear-eyed truth bomb. Bright enough to see every expression.

### Audio
- **Mic:** Lapel mic. Clean audio essential for the controlled intensity in delivery.
- **Environment:** Quiet room. No background noise.

### Editing
- **Captions:** Hardcoded, bold white with dark shadow. Full transcript.
- **Text overlays:** Snap in (not fade) -- this ad has more edge than others in the batch. The overlays should match the energy.
- **Cuts:** One or two jump cuts to tighten pacing. This ad moves fast. If there's a stumble, cut it. The momentum matters.
- **Color grade:** Neutral-warm. Not heavily graded. Clean and honest. Slightly cooler than Concept 11 but not cold.
- **Music:** None. Or the barest hint of tension in the background -- a low drone that resolves to warmth at the bridge. If it feels manufactured, kill it.
- **Pacing:** Second-fastest ad in the batch after Concept 04. The hook is dense. The problem section builds. Then the mechanism slows slightly for the relief. CTA is punchy.
- **End frame:** CTA overlay holds 3 seconds.
