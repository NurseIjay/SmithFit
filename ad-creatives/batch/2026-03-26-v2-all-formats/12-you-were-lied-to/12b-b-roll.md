# Concept 12: You Were Lied To -- B-Roll
**Funnel Stage:** TOF (Cold)
**Format:** B-Roll with IJ Voiceover
**Duration:** 28-32 seconds
**Objective:** Contrast generic fitness advice visuals with real nurse-life footage, channeling anger at the system

---

## Shot List

### SHOT 1 -- Scrolling Generic Fitness Content (Hook)
- **What to film:** Close-up of a thumb scrolling through Instagram. Screen shows bright, polished fitness posts: "5 AM workout routine!" "Clean eating meal prep!" "Rise and grind!" Content that's clearly made for people with normal schedules.
- **Camera angle:** Over-the-shoulder close-up on phone screen
- **Movement:** Thumb scrolling slowly, pausing briefly on each post
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Phone screen glow in a dim room. She's scrolling after a shift.
- **Props:** Phone with mock Instagram feed loaded (bright, generic fitness content)

### SHOT 2 -- Clock Showing 7:12 PM (Shift Start)
- **What to film:** Close-up of a car dashboard clock showing 7:12 PM. A coffee thermos sits in the cupholder. A badge lanyard hangs from the rearview mirror. She's heading in.
- **Camera angle:** Close-up from passenger seat, focused on clock and thermos
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 2 seconds
- **Lighting:** Dusk light through windshield. Golden-orange fading.
- **Props:** Car dashboard, coffee thermos, badge lanyard, dusk sky visible

### SHOT 3 -- Break Room at Midnight
- **What to film:** Overhead shot of a break room table: a vending machine bag of chips, a half-eaten granola bar, a cold cup of coffee. A phone shows 12:22 AM. This is "meal prep" when you work nights.
- **Camera angle:** Overhead bird's eye
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 2.5 seconds
- **Lighting:** Harsh fluorescent overhead. Institutional. Unflattering.
- **Props:** Vending machine chips, granola bar, cold coffee, phone showing time, institutional table surface

### SHOT 4 -- 3 AM Charting
- **What to film:** Close-up of hands typing on a keyboard at a desk. A computer screen glows. The clock on the wall says 3:07 AM. A snack wrapper sits crumpled beside the keyboard. This is the moment nobody sees.
- **Camera angle:** Close-up from the side, focused on hands and the crumpled wrapper
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 2.5 seconds
- **Lighting:** Computer screen glow and dim ambient light. Blue-toned. Exhausting.
- **Props:** Keyboard, computer screen, wall clock or phone showing 3 AM, crumpled snack wrapper

### SHOT 5 -- Driving Home at Dawn
- **What to film:** Shot from inside a car. Windshield view of an empty road at dawn. Sun coming up. She's driving home while everyone else is waking up. A takeout bag sits on the passenger seat.
- **Camera angle:** Dashboard POV, facing forward through windshield
- **Movement:** Static (car in motion or parked with dawn visible)
- **Duration:** 2.5 seconds
- **Lighting:** Dawn light. Pink-orange sky. Beautiful but lonely.
- **Props:** Car interior, takeout bag on passenger seat, dawn sky

### SHOT 6 -- Magazine/Ad Ripped in Half (The Break)
- **What to film:** A printed page with "EAT LESS. MOVE MORE." in bold generic font, being crumpled or torn in half by a hand. Slow, deliberate. Not violent -- decisive. She's done.
- **Camera angle:** Close-up on the paper and hand, overhead
- **Movement:** Static, hand enters frame
- **Duration:** 2 seconds
- **Lighting:** Warm kitchen light
- **Props:** Printed paper with generic diet advice, hand

### SHOT 7 -- IJ Writing "SHIFT-BASED" on Whiteboard
- **What to film:** IJ's hand crossing out "9-to-5 RULES" on a whiteboard and writing "SHIFT-BASED" below it, then circling it.
- **Camera angle:** Close-up on whiteboard, hand and marker visible
- **Movement:** Following the hand
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Clean, warm
- **Props:** Whiteboard, dry-erase marker, "9-to-5 RULES" pre-written

### SHOT 8 -- IJ to Camera (Bridge)
- **What to film:** IJ speaking directly to camera. Scrubs on. Confident, calm. The face behind the voiceover.
- **Camera angle:** Chest-up, eye level, straight-on
- **Movement:** Static (tripod)
- **Duration:** 4 seconds
- **Lighting:** Natural window light, warm
- **Props:** Scrubs, clean background

### SHOT 9 -- Nurse Walking Forward with Purpose (Close)
- **What to film:** Medium shot of someone in scrubs walking toward camera with good posture and intention. Not exhausted. Ready. The "after" energy -- but it's not about looks. It's about direction.
- **Camera angle:** Medium, straight-on, slightly low (empowering)
- **Movement:** Figure walks toward stationary camera
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Bright daylight. Clean and warm.
- **Props:** Scrubs or casual, outdoor or hallway

## Complete Filming Guide

### Summary
- **Total shots:** 9
- **Estimated filming time:** 50-65 minutes
- **Locations needed:** 3 (car, home interior, exterior)
- **Wardrobe:** Scrubs

### Props Checklist
- [ ] Phone with mock Instagram fitness feed
- [ ] Car with visible dashboard clock
- [ ] Coffee thermos
- [ ] Badge lanyard
- [ ] Vending machine chips, granola bar, cold coffee cup
- [ ] Phone showing 12:22 AM
- [ ] Institutional table or surface
- [ ] Keyboard, computer screen (or laptop)
- [ ] Wall clock or phone showing 3 AM
- [ ] Crumpled snack wrapper
- [ ] Takeout bag
- [ ] Printed paper with "EAT LESS. MOVE MORE."
- [ ] Whiteboard, dry-erase marker, "9-to-5 RULES" pre-written
- [ ] Scrubs (dark navy/charcoal)

### Location Checklist
- [ ] Car interior (dusk and dawn shots)
- [ ] Break room setup (kitchen table with fluorescent light works)
- [ ] Desk area with keyboard and lamp/computer glow
- [ ] Kitchen (paper-ripping shot)
- [ ] Clean background room (whiteboard, IJ to camera)
- [ ] Outdoor or hallway (walk-forward shot)

### Editing Notes
- **Cut pacing:** Shots 1-5 build tension. Each cut is slightly faster than the last. Shot 6 (paper ripped) is the release point -- hold it one beat longer. Shots 7-9 are the resolution -- calmer, more confident.
- **Color grade:** Shots 1-5: slightly desaturated, cool blue tones (the unfairness, the exhaustion). Shot 6: neutral (the turning point). Shots 7-9: warm, bright (the alternative).
- **Captions:** Full hardcoded. Bold white, lower third.
- **Music:** Subtle tension build through Shots 1-5 -- a low pulse or drone. Brief silence at Shot 6 (the break). Warm resolution music Shots 7-9. Think documentary, not cinematic thriller.
- **Sound design:** Optional -- phone scroll sounds (Shot 1), keyboard clicks (Shot 4), paper tearing (Shot 6).
