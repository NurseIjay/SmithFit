# Concept 14: The Comparison Trap -- B-Roll
**Funnel Stage:** TOF (Cold)
**Format:** B-Roll with IJ Voiceover
**Duration:** 28-32 seconds
**Objective:** Visual storytelling of the comparison moment, then the relief of understanding why her situation is different

---

## Shot List

### SHOT 1 -- Break Room Table, Two Meals Side by Side (Hook)
- **What to film:** Overhead shot of a break room table. On one side: a neatly packed meal prep container with colorful food, a water bottle, a protein bar. On the other side: a vending machine bag of chips, a granola bar, a cold coffee cup. Two lunches. Two realities. The camera lingers on the contrast.
- **Camera angle:** Overhead bird's eye, centered on both meals
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Fluorescent overhead. Break room reality.
- **Props:** Meal prep container (neat, colorful), water bottle, protein bar, vending machine chips, granola bar, cold coffee, institutional table

### SHOT 2 -- Scrubs in a Mirror (Her View)
- **What to film:** Medium shot of a person standing in front of a mirror in scrubs, pulling at the waist. The gesture is subtle -- not dramatic. Just a private moment of noticing how things fit.
- **Camera angle:** Over-the-shoulder into mirror
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 2.5 seconds
- **Lighting:** Bathroom vanity light, warm
- **Props:** Scrubs, bathroom mirror

### SHOT 3 -- Clock Showing 2:00 AM
- **What to film:** Close-up of a phone screen showing 2:03 AM. It's on a desk next to a half-eaten protein bar. She's on shift. This is her "normal."
- **Camera angle:** Close-up, shallow depth of field
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 2 seconds
- **Lighting:** Phone glow and dim ambient
- **Props:** Phone showing time, protein bar, desk surface

### SHOT 4 -- Hands Gripping a Coffee Thermos
- **What to film:** Close-up of tired hands wrapped around a coffee thermos. No rings visible (too tight to wear). The hands are still -- a moment of pause in a 12-hour shift.
- **Camera angle:** Tight close-up on hands and thermos
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 2.5 seconds
- **Lighting:** Natural or fluorescent ambient
- **Props:** Coffee thermos, bare ring finger (deliberate detail)

### SHOT 5 -- Kitchen Counter: Her Real Life
- **What to film:** Wide shot of a kitchen counter at night. A lunchbox sits next to a child's homework. A work bag is open, half unpacked. A microwave dinner is heating. This is the "after shift" reality -- not Instagram-worthy meal prep.
- **Camera angle:** Medium-wide, slightly high angle
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Warm kitchen light, lived-in
- **Props:** Lunchbox, child's homework papers, open work bag, microwave or microwave dinner container

### SHOT 6 -- Two Hands on a Whiteboard (The Explanation)
- **What to film:** IJ's hand drawing two simple lines on a whiteboard. One labeled "HER" stays flat. One labeled "YOU" is wavy and elevated. He circles the elevated line and writes "CORTISOL" with an arrow.
- **Camera angle:** Close-up on whiteboard
- **Movement:** Following the hand
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Clean, warm
- **Props:** Whiteboard, dry-erase markers (black and gold/red)

### SHOT 7 -- IJ to Camera (The Relief)
- **What to film:** IJ speaking directly to camera. Warm, certain expression. This is the moment he tells her nothing is wrong with her.
- **Camera angle:** Chest-up, eye level, close (1.5-2 feet)
- **Movement:** Static (tripod)
- **Duration:** 5 seconds
- **Lighting:** Natural window light, warm
- **Props:** Scrubs or casual, clean background

### SHOT 8 -- Morning Light Through Window (Hope)
- **What to film:** Slow shot of morning sunlight coming through a kitchen window. A clear glass of water with lemon sits on the windowsill. Quiet. Calm. A new start.
- **Camera angle:** Medium, focused on the glass and the light
- **Movement:** Slow drift right
- **Duration:** 2.5 seconds
- **Lighting:** Golden morning light. Warm, hopeful.
- **Props:** Clear glass with lemon, windowsill, morning light

### SHOT 9 -- Walking with Purpose (Close)
- **What to film:** Medium shot of someone walking forward with good posture. Scrubs or casual. Not a power walk -- just a person who looks like she figured something out. Calm, grounded.
- **Camera angle:** Medium, straight-on, slightly low
- **Movement:** Figure walks toward camera
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Bright natural light
- **Props:** Scrubs or casual outfit

## Complete Filming Guide

### Summary
- **Total shots:** 9
- **Estimated filming time:** 45-55 minutes
- **Locations needed:** 3 (home interior, bathroom, exterior)
- **Wardrobe:** Scrubs + casual

### Props Checklist
- [ ] Meal prep container (neat, colorful food)
- [ ] Water bottle, protein bar
- [ ] Vending machine chips, granola bar, cold coffee
- [ ] Institutional table or surface
- [ ] Scrubs, bathroom mirror
- [ ] Phone showing 2:03 AM
- [ ] Half-eaten protein bar
- [ ] Coffee thermos
- [ ] Lunchbox, child's homework papers, work bag, microwave dinner
- [ ] Whiteboard, dry-erase markers (black and gold/red)
- [ ] Clear glass with lemon, windowsill with morning light
- [ ] Clean casual outfit for walk shot

### Location Checklist
- [ ] Break room setup (kitchen table with fluorescent light)
- [ ] Bathroom mirror
- [ ] Desk area (night shift charting)
- [ ] Kitchen (night reality shot, morning hope shot)
- [ ] Clean background (whiteboard, IJ to camera)
- [ ] Outdoor or hallway (walk forward)

### Editing Notes
- **Cut pacing:** Shots 1-2 are slow, setting the comparison. Shot 3 is the pivot -- "nothing is wrong with you" needs a beat. Shots 4-5 show her real life at medium pace. Shot 6 (whiteboard) is the education moment. Shot 7 (IJ to camera) lingers -- the reframe lives here. Shots 8-9 lift and close.
- **Color grade:** Shots 1-5: slightly cooler, slightly muted (the weight of comparison). Shot 6: neutral (education). Shots 7-9: warm, golden (relief, hope).
- **The contrast in Shot 1:** The neat meal prep side should look almost too perfect. The vending machine side should look real. The visual does the comparison work.
- **Captions:** Full hardcoded. Bold white, lower third.
- **Music:** Very soft, reflective tone for Shots 1-5. Lifts warmly at Shot 7. Think "realization" music -- gentle, not sentimental.
