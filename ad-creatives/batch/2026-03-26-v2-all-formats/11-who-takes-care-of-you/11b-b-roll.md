# Concept 11: Who Takes Care of You? -- B-Roll
**Funnel Stage:** TOF (Cold)
**Format:** B-Roll with IJ Voiceover
**Duration:** 30-35 seconds
**Objective:** Cinematic nurse-life visuals showing the caregiver's day -- everyone she serves before herself

---

## Shot List

### SHOT 1 -- Hands Adjusting Patient's Blanket (Hook)
- **What to film:** Close-up of hands in medical gloves gently adjusting a blanket over a pillow on a bed. Careful, tender movement. The hands of someone who gives care all day.
- **Camera angle:** Tight close-up on hands, 45-degree overhead
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 2.5 seconds
- **Lighting:** Cool fluorescent -- institutional. Clinical feel.
- **Props:** Medical gloves (nitrile), blanket, bed or couch styled to look like a patient bed

### SHOT 2 -- Badge Swinging While Walking
- **What to film:** Close-up of a badge lanyard swinging against scrubs while someone walks briskly. The purposeful walk of a nurse mid-shift. Badge shows "RN."
- **Camera angle:** Chest-level close-up from the side, tracking the badge
- **Movement:** Slow tracking alongside
- **Duration:** 2 seconds
- **Lighting:** Fluorescent hallway light
- **Props:** Badge lanyard with RN credential, scrubs

### SHOT 3 -- Coming Home to Kids
- **What to film:** Medium shot from inside a house looking at the front door. The door opens and a figure in scrubs enters. A child's shoes and a backpack are visible by the door. A lunch box sits on the counter. The door barely closes before the day continues.
- **Camera angle:** Medium-wide from inside, facing the door
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Warm interior home light. Contrast to the clinical shots before.
- **Props:** Scrubs, front door, child's shoes/backpack, lunch box on counter

### SHOT 4 -- Hands Packing a Lunch
- **What to film:** Close-up of hands packing a small lunch box. Putting in a sandwich, a juice box, a napkin with a little heart drawn on it. She's still taking care of someone -- just a smaller someone now.
- **Camera angle:** Overhead tight close-up
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 2.5 seconds
- **Lighting:** Warm kitchen light
- **Props:** Small lunch box, sandwich, juice box, napkin with drawn heart, kitchen counter

### SHOT 5 -- Sitting Alone at the Kitchen Table
- **What to film:** Medium-wide shot of a kitchen table. One person sits alone. Everyone else is in bed. A cold plate of food sits untouched. Phone on the table. The quiet after everyone else is taken care of.
- **Camera angle:** Medium from across the table, slightly low
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Single kitchen light. Everything else dim. Isolating.
- **Props:** Kitchen table, untouched plate of food, phone, mug, dim lighting

### SHOT 6 -- Mirror Check Before Bed
- **What to film:** Medium shot of a woman's reflection in a bathroom mirror. She's changed out of scrubs into a t-shirt. She pauses, looks at herself. Turns to the side briefly. A quiet, private moment with her own body.
- **Camera angle:** Over-the-shoulder into mirror, slightly off-center
- **Movement:** Static
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Warm bathroom vanity light
- **Props:** Bathroom mirror, casual clothes (t-shirt), tired expression

### SHOT 7 -- Morning Cocktail (The Reset)
- **What to film:** Close-up of hands mixing the metabolic cocktail -- water, lemon, pinch of salt in a clear glass. Sunlight streaming through a kitchen window. Calm, intentional, quiet. This is the first thing she's done for herself.
- **Camera angle:** Medium close-up, 45-degree angle, focused on hands and glass
- **Movement:** Slight handheld drift (organic, human)
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Golden morning sunlight through window. Warm. Hopeful. Sharp contrast to the dim kitchen table shot.
- **Props:** Clear glass, lemon wedge, small salt container, clean counter, morning light

### SHOT 8 -- IJ to Camera (Bridge)
- **What to film:** IJ speaking directly to camera. Calm, genuine. This is the "face" moment where voiceover connects to a real person.
- **Camera angle:** Chest-up, straight-on, eye level
- **Movement:** Static (tripod)
- **Duration:** 4 seconds
- **Lighting:** Warm natural window light
- **Props:** Clean background, casual clothes

### SHOT 9 -- Walking Into Daylight (Close)
- **What to film:** Medium shot of a figure pushing open a door and stepping into bright morning sunlight. Posture up. Not in scrubs -- in her own clothes, on her own time. A moment that's just for her.
- **Camera angle:** Medium from slightly below, facing the person as they walk toward camera
- **Movement:** Static, figure walks toward lens
- **Duration:** 3 seconds
- **Lighting:** Bright warm daylight flooding through doorway. Slight lens flare.
- **Props:** Door, casual clothes, sunlight

## Complete Filming Guide

### Summary
- **Total shots:** 9
- **Estimated filming time:** 45-60 minutes
- **Locations needed:** 3 (home interior, bathroom, exterior door)
- **Wardrobe:** Scrubs + casual home clothes

### Props Checklist
- [ ] Medical gloves (nitrile)
- [ ] Blanket and pillow (or bed)
- [ ] Badge lanyard with RN ID
- [ ] Scrubs (dark navy/charcoal)
- [ ] Child's shoes and small backpack
- [ ] Small lunch box, sandwich, juice box, napkin
- [ ] Kitchen table, plate of food, phone, mug
- [ ] Bathroom mirror
- [ ] Casual clothes (t-shirt for mirror shot, clean outfit for walk-out)
- [ ] Clear glass, lemon wedge, salt container
- [ ] Door to exterior

### Location Checklist
- [ ] Kitchen (table scene, lunch packing, morning cocktail)
- [ ] Front door area (coming home shot)
- [ ] Bathroom mirror
- [ ] Hallway (badge walking)
- [ ] Exterior door (walk-out to daylight)

### Editing Notes
- **Cut pacing:** Shots 1-5 are slow, steady, building the weight of her day. Each shot lingers. Shot 5 (alone at table) is the emotional fulcrum -- hold it. Shots 6-9 gradually lift.
- **Color grade:** Shots 1-2: cool, clinical, fluorescent feel. Shots 3-6: warm but dim, muted -- the home she returns to. Shot 7 onward: golden, bright, hopeful.
- **The pause:** Between "everyone gets the best of you" and "who takes care of you?" -- two full beats of silence. Let the image of her sitting alone at the kitchen table carry the weight. No music during this beat.
- **Captions:** Full hardcoded. Bold white, lower third.
- **Music:** Soft, minimal piano. Barely audible through Shots 1-5. Drops to silence during the pause. Returns warmer for Shots 7-9. Never louder than voiceover.
- **Sound design:** Optional -- quiet sound of a door closing (Shot 3), glass clinking (Shot 7).
