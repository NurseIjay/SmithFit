# Concept 11: Who Takes Care of You? -- Talking Head
**Funnel Stage:** TOF (Cold)
**Format:** Talking Head (IJ to camera)
**Duration:** 28-35 seconds
**Objective:** Tap into the caregiver identity -- the #1 missing emotional angle -- and make her feel seen for the first time

---

## Script (Word-for-Word)

### HOOK (0-4 sec)
**IJ says:** "You spend 12 hours taking care of patients. Then you come home and take care of your kids. Your family. Everyone gets the best of you."

**On-screen text overlay:** "Everyone gets the best of you."
**IJ's energy/expression:** Calm, measured, looking directly into her eyes. Not sad -- knowing. Like he's describing her Tuesday. Slight pause after "everyone gets the best of you." Let it land.

---

### THE QUESTION (4-7 sec)
**IJ says:** "But who takes care of you?"

**On-screen text overlay:** "Who takes care of YOU?"
**IJ's energy/expression:** This is the emotional peak. Deliver it quietly. Not dramatic. Almost gentle. Like a friend who finally asked the question nobody asks. Hold the pause after. Two full beats of silence. Let her feel it.

---

### MECHANISM (7-16 sec)
**IJ says:** "Your body has been in fight-or-flight for years. Not because something's wrong with you. Because you never stop. You never get to rest. And your cortisol -- your stress hormones -- they've been running on overdrive this whole time. That's why the weight won't move. That's why you're exhausted even on your days off."

**On-screen text overlay:** "Your stress hormones have been in overdrive for years." (appears at 9 sec)
**IJ's energy/expression:** Shift from emotional to clear-eyed explanation. Not clinical -- plain-spoken. "Your stress hormones" not "cortisol levels." He's explaining something he figured out the hard way. Lean in slightly on "that's why the weight won't move."

---

### THE REFRAME (16-22 sec)
**IJ says:** "I'm a nurse. I lived this. And I built something that's not another diet. It's not another workout plan. It's the first thing that's just for you."

**On-screen text overlay:** "The first thing that's just for you."
**IJ's energy/expression:** Warm conviction. "I'm a nurse" is stated simply -- not as a credential flex, as solidarity. "The first thing that's just for you" lands with genuine care. He means it.

---

### CTA (22-30 sec)
**IJ says:** "The Shift-Proof Metabolism Reset. $27. 60-day money-back guarantee. What if you gave yourself the same care you give your patients? Link in bio."

**On-screen text overlay:** "$27 | 60-day guarantee | Link in bio"
**IJ's energy/expression:** Direct but soft. The question "what if you gave yourself the same care" is not rhetorical fluff -- deliver it like a real question. Hold eye contact. Slight nod at the end.

## A/B Hook Variations

### Variation A (Default -- above)
"You spend 12 hours taking care of patients. Then you come home and take care of your kids. Your family. Everyone gets the best of you."

### Variation B (Shorter, punchier)
"Twelve hours for your patients. Then your kids. Your family. Your house. And at the end of the day -- what's left for you? Nothing."
**On-screen text:** "What's left for you?"

### Variation C (Confession angle)
"Can I ask you something that might hit different? When's the last time you did something that was just for you? Not for your patients. Not for your kids. Just you."
**On-screen text:** "When's the last time?"

## Production Notes

### Setting
- **Location:** Living room or kitchen. Warm, home environment. NOT clinical.
- **Background:** Slightly lived-in -- a couch, a blanket, family photos barely visible in the blur. This is her world.
- **Vibe:** Intimate. Like he came over after she got off shift and they're sitting at the kitchen table.

### Wardrobe
- **Option 1 (preferred):** Clean casual -- solid color tee, maybe a hoodie unzipped. This isn't a coaching ad. This is a human moment.
- **Option 2:** Scrubs with the top unbuttoned or a casual layer over scrub pants. The "just got off shift" look.

### Camera
- **Device:** iPhone on tripod
- **Framing:** Chest-up, centered, vertical (9:16 / 1080x1920)
- **Angle:** Eye level. Straight on. Close -- 1.5-2 feet. This should feel like a conversation, not a broadcast.
- **Distance:** Intimate. Closer than other ads in the batch.

### Lighting
- **Primary:** Warm lamp or window light. Golden, soft.
- **Avoid:** Anything harsh or clinical. This is the warmest ad in the batch.
- **Time of day:** Golden hour or warm evening lamp light.

### Audio
- **Mic:** Lapel mic clipped to shirt. Clean audio critical -- the quiet delivery demands it.
- **Environment:** Dead quiet. No background noise. The silence between lines IS the production value.

### Editing
- **Captions:** Hardcoded, bold white with dark shadow. Full transcript. Slightly smaller font than other ads -- the intimacy should carry to the text.
- **Text overlays:** Fade in gently, not snap. One at a time. Hold longer than other ads.
- **Cuts:** Zero. Single take. The unbroken eye contact and the pause after "who takes care of you?" are the entire creative. Do not cut away from his face during that pause.
- **Color grade:** Warm golden. Soft contrast. The warmest grade in the batch.
- **Music:** Very soft piano. Barely there. Starts after "who takes care of you?" -- supports the emotion without manipulating it. If it feels manipulative, remove it entirely.
- **Pacing:** Slowest ad in the batch. Let every pause breathe. The silence does the work.
- **End frame:** CTA overlay holds 4 seconds.
