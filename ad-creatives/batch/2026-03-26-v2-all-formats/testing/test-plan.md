# SmithFit Ad Testing Plan
**Batch:** 2026-03-26-v2-all-formats
**Total:** 10 concepts x 4 formats = 40 ad variations (80 with A/B hooks)
**Updated:** 2026-03-26

---

## Testing Philosophy

1. **Test ONE variable at a time.** Never change hook + visual + CTA simultaneously.
2. **Need 1,000+ impressions** before drawing conclusions.
3. **CPA (cost per $27 purchase) is the winning metric**, not CTR alone.
4. **Kill fast, scale slow.** Cut losers after 3 days/$15. Scale winners 20-30% every 3 days.
5. **Format-level testing is new in this batch.** We're testing talking head vs. B-roll vs. static vs. carousel for each concept.

## Format-Level Testing (New for This Batch)

### The Key Question
For each concept, which format performs best? This batch lets us answer:

| Test | What We Learn | How to Measure |
|------|--------------|----------------|
| Talking head vs. B-roll (Concept 01) | Does IJ's face or cinematic footage drive more engagement? | CTR, video completion, CPA |
| Video vs. Static (Concept 02) | Does video outperform static for the validation hook? | CTR, CPA |
| Video vs. Carousel (Concept 03) | Does swipeable content outperform video for identity hooks? | CTR, CPA, swipe-through rate |
| Static vs. Carousel (Concepts 08, 09) | Which format converts better for BOF direct-offer? | CPA, ROAS |

### How to Test
- Launch both formats simultaneously to the SAME audience
- Split budget evenly between formats
- After 7 days, compare CPA and ROAS
- Scale the winning format; pause the losing format
- Deploy the winning format for all remaining concepts in that funnel stage

## A/B Hook Testing

Each concept has 2 hook variations. Test them:

### Method
1. Create 2 ad sets per concept: identical targeting, different creative (Variation A vs B)
2. Run for 5-7 days at equal budget
3. Winner = lower CPA (not higher CTR -- CPA is what matters)
4. Kill the loser, scale the winner

### Hook Tests by Concept

| Concept | Hook A | Hook B | Hypothesis |
|---------|--------|--------|-----------|
| 01: Ghost | Origin story | Confession opener | Story hooks typically outperform confessions for cold traffic |
| 02: Not Lazy | Validation ("not lazy") | Question ("what if I told you") | Validation likely wins -- it's the #1 pattern |
| 03: Night Shift | "Stop blaming yourself" | "Made for 9-to-5. You don't work 9-to-5." | Identity hook likely outperforms command hook |
| 04: Coffee | "Coffee and vibes" | "I'm talking to you" | Avatar language likely wins on engagement; direct address may win on CPA |
| 05: Sarah | Meet Sarah (story) | 30 lbs eating MORE (result) | Result-first likely wins for warm traffic |
| 06: Diet Fails | "Why your diet fails" | "3.6 million nurses" | Curiosity hook likely outperforms stat opener |
| 07: Objection | "Handle your objections" | "Still on the fence?" | Retargeting specific language likely wins for warm traffic |
| 08: Quiz | "What's your stress type?" | "I can tell you why in 2 minutes" | Challenge hook may drive more clicks; category hook may drive more completions |
| 09: DoorDash | DoorDash comparison | Starbucks comparison | DoorDash likely resonates more (post-shift specific) |
| 10: Feel | "Feel like yourself" | "Imagine scrubs fitting" | Aspirational identity likely wins over specific descriptor |

## Weekly Reporting Cadence

### Day 3 Check (Kill/Continue)
- Pull CTR for all active ads
- Kill anything below 0.3% CTR with $15+ spend
- Note any standout performers (CTR > 1%)

### Day 7 Report
- Full metrics: CTR, CPC, CPA, ROAS, video completion, carousel swipe-through
- Compare format-level performance
- Compare A/B hook performance
- Scale winners. Kill losers.
- Plan Wave 2 deployment based on learnings

### Day 14 Report
- Same metrics + audience insights (who's converting?)
- Build retargeting audiences from video viewers and engagers
- Deploy Wave 2 concepts
- Calculate blended CPA across all ads

### Day 30 Report
- Full funnel analysis: CPL (quiz) -> CPA ($27) -> upsell rate ($67/mo)
- Identify top 3 performing ads overall
- Plan creative refresh for fatigued ads
- Expand to Lookalike audiences if data permits
