# Concept 06: Why Your Diet Fails -- Talking Head
**Funnel Stage:** MOF (Warm)
**Format:** Talking Head (IJ to camera, personal revelation -- NOT a lecture)
**Duration:** 34-40 seconds
**Objective:** Deep mechanism education that FEELS like IJ sharing something personal, anger at bad advice, counterintuitive hook

---

## Script (Word-for-Word)

### HOOK (0-4 sec)
**IJ says:** "Can I be honest with you about something? Every piece of diet advice you've gotten was designed for someone with a 9-to-5 schedule and weekends off. You were set up to fail."

**On-screen text overlay:** "You were set up to fail."
**IJ's energy/expression:** Not calm educator -- frustrated friend. This is the anger hook. "You were set up to fail" -- direct eye contact, slight lean forward. He's mad on her behalf.

---

### PROBLEM SETUP (4-12 sec)
**IJ says:** "Here's what nobody tells you. When you work a 12-hour shift -- especially nights -- your stress hormones stay elevated the entire time. Not a spike. Twelve straight hours of cortisol. Day after day. Week after week. And here's the part that made me angry when I figured it out..."

**On-screen text overlay:** "12-hour shift = 12 hours of cortisol" (at 6 sec)
**IJ's energy/expression:** Building intensity. Not lecturing -- confiding. "The part that made me angry" -- this is personal for IJ. He discovered this for himself. Let the viewer feel that.

---

### MECHANISM + COUNTERINTUITIVE (12-22 sec)
**IJ says:** "...eating LESS makes cortisol WORSE. You read that right. When you cut calories and your body is already stressed, cortisol goes higher. Your body stores more fat. Your cravings get worse. Your metabolism slows down. So every diet you tried? It was pouring gas on the fire. And nobody told you."

**On-screen text overlay:** "Eating LESS makes cortisol WORSE." (at 13 sec) then "Survival mode: stores fat, slows metabolism, ramps cravings" (at 17 sec)
**IJ's energy/expression:** This is the revelation moment. "Eating LESS makes cortisol WORSE" -- slow, deliberate, let it sink in. "Pouring gas on the fire" -- slight shake of the head. "Nobody told you" -- empathy.

---

**On-screen text overlay:** "Calm the stress response first. Then everything works." (at 24 sec)
**IJ's energy/expression:** The relief turn. Warmer. "Calm your nervous system down first" -- said like a prescription from a nurse who cares. Not a pitch. A recommendation.

## Production Notes

### Setting
- Clean, simple background. Home office or neutral wall. Authority setting -- not the kitchen, not the gym.

### Wardrobe
- Scrubs (preferred -- reinforces RN credential). Badge visible. Clean, professional.

### Camera
- iPhone on tripod. Vertical 9:16. Chest-up. Eye level. 2-3 feet.

### Lighting
- Natural window light or clean ring light. Bright, even. This is an educational piece -- no moody lighting.

### Audio
- Lapel mic. Quiet room. This ad has the most words -- clear audio is critical.

### Editing
- **Captions:** Hardcoded, full transcript. Bold white, dark shadow. Critical for this ad -- 85% will watch without sound and this is an info-dense script.
- **Text overlays:** As noted. Key revelations get larger, gold treatment.
- **Cuts:** 2-4 jump cuts to tighten. Remove any ums, pauses, or repeated phrases.
- **Color grade:** Bright, warm, consistent. No mood shifts in this ad.
- **Music:** None. The anger and revelation carry the energy. Silence makes the counterintuitive moment hit harder.
- **Pacing note:** This is the longest talking head in the batch. IJ should rehearse until it flows naturally. The counterintuitive section (12-22 sec) must feel like a personal revelation, not a lecture.
