## THE HOOK (0-3 seconds)

**On-screen text:** "38. Nurse. 3 kids. Adopting a 4th."

**Sarah says:** "I'm Sarah. I'm 38. I'm a nurse. I have 3 kids and we're adopting our 4th."

**Visual:** Sarah filmed on her phone. Natural lighting. Casual setting -- kitchen, living room, car. NOT staged. The rawness is the quality signal.

**Sound:** Sarah's voice only. No music.

---

## THE PROBLEM (3-12 seconds)

**Sarah says:** "One of my kids has autism and requires full transfers. So I need to be strong for that. But I was exhausted. I'd tried every diet -- I even did 1,000 calories a day at one point. Nothing worked with my schedule. I was starting to think my body was broken."

**Visual:** Same shot. Sarah is conversational, not rehearsed. She can look away from the camera, pause, be real. Imperfection makes it believable.

**On-screen text:** "Tried everything. Even 1,000 calories a day."

---

## THE DISCOVERY (12-20 seconds)

**Sarah says:** "I found SmithFit because IJ is actually a nurse. He gets what our shifts do to our bodies. He told me it wasn't about eating less -- it was about calming my cortisol first. So I started eating MORE. And it worked."

**Visual:** Same continuous shot. Her expression shifts when she says "it worked" -- this is the emotional turn.

**On-screen text:** "Started eating MORE."

---

## THE RESULT (20-30 seconds)

**Sarah says:** "I lost nearly 30 pounds in 8 and a half weeks. Eating more than before. My resting heart rate dropped 12 to 15 beats per minute. I can transfer my son confidently now. I feel like myself again."

**Visual:** Same shot. Sarah's energy lifts -- she's proud, not performing. If she tears up or smiles genuinely, that's gold. Don't re-shoot it.

**On-screen text (timed):**
- At "30 pounds": "Nearly 30 lbs in 8.5 weeks"
- At "resting heart rate": "Resting HR down 12-15 BPM"
- At "feel like myself": "I feel like myself again."

**Sarah says:** "It's $27. If I can do it with 3 kids, adopting a 4th, and working 12-hour shifts -- you can too."

**Visual:** Sarah looks at camera. Direct, warm.

**On-screen text (final frame, hold 3 seconds):**

## PRODUCTION NOTES

**This ad requires Sarah (or a client with a comparable story) to film herself. Below are the instructions to send to the client.**

### Filming Instructions for Sarah

> Hey Sarah -- we'd love to share your story to inspire other nurses. Here's all you need to do:
>
> 1. **Film on your phone.** Hold it vertical. Prop it up or use a selfie stick.
> 2. **Find good light.** Face a window during the day, or sit in a well-lit room.
> 3. **Talk to the camera like you're talking to a nurse friend.** You don't need to memorize anything. Tell your story in your own words. Hit these points:
>    - Who you are (name, age, that you're a nurse, your kids)
>    - What you tried before that didn't work
>    - What was different about SmithFit
>    - How you feel now
>    - What you'd tell a nurse who's thinking about trying it
> 4. **Keep it 30-45 seconds.** Don't worry about being perfect. Real is better than polished.
> 5. **Send us the raw video.** We'll add captions and text overlays.
>
> Thank you for helping other nurses hear this.

**Wardrobe:** Whatever Sarah is comfortable in. Scrubs would be a bonus but not required.

**Energy level:** Authentic. Not rehearsed, not scripted word-for-word. The script above is a DIRECTION, not a teleprompter. Sarah's own words will be more powerful.

**Editing notes:**
- Auto-captions (critical -- 85%+ watch without sound)
- Text overlays for key stats (30 lbs, resting HR, 8.5 weeks)
- "Results may vary" must appear both on-screen and in the caption
- Trim dead air but keep natural pauses -- they build authenticity
- Do NOT add music. Do NOT add branded graphics beyond the final CTA slide.

## A/B VARIATIONS

### Variation A -- "I Was Eating 1,000 Calories" Hook
**Alternate opening:** Sarah leads with: "I was eating 1,000 calories a day and GAINING weight."
**On-screen text:** "1,000 calories. Still gaining."
**This hook leads with the most counterintuitive detail first.**

### Variation B -- "My Son" Hook
**Alternate opening:** Sarah leads with: "My son has autism and needs me to lift him. I wasn't strong enough. That's what made me try one more thing."
**On-screen text:** "I need to be strong for my son."
**This hook leads with the emotional WHY, not the physical result.**

### Variation C -- IJ Introduces, Sarah Tells
**Alternate structure:** IJ opens with 3-second intro: "This is Sarah. She's a nurse, a mom of 3, adopting a 4th. Here's her story in her own words." Then cut to Sarah's UGC.
**Tests whether IJ's authority as the intro boosts credibility for Sarah's story.**
