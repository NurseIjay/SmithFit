## PRODUCTION NOTES

**Design tool:** Canva or Figma using SmithFit brand kit.

**Sizes needed:**
- 1080x1080 (Instagram/Facebook feed -- primary)
- 1080x1920 (Instagram Stories -- resize for Stories placement)

**Brand aesthetic:** Gold, cream, elegant. Clean typography (sans-serif for headlines, serif accent optional for quotes). No photos -- text-driven proof. High contrast for readability.

**Typography:**
- Headlines: Bold, 48-60pt equivalent
- Quotes: Regular or light, 28-36pt equivalent, with quotation marks
- Attribution: Italic, 18-24pt
- CTA text: Bold, 36-48pt

**Color palette:**
- Primary: Gold (#C5A55A or similar warm gold)
- Secondary: Cream (#FFF8F0 or similar warm white)
- Text: Dark charcoal (#2D2D2D) or white, depending on background
- Accent: Muted gold line or border

**Important:** Replace testimonial names with actual client names once permission is collected. The quotes above are based on real SmithFit outcomes -- replace with verbatim client quotes when available.

## A/B VARIATIONS

### Variation A -- Photo Testimonials
**Same quotes but add a photo element to each testimonial slide:**
- Client's photo (headshot or candid, with permission)
- Or: a stylized icon/avatar silhouette if photos unavailable
**Tests whether faces increase trust and CTR on carousel slides.**

### Variation B -- Stat-Led Slide Order
**Reorder slides:**
- Slide 1: "87% feel different by Day 3" (stat hook)
- Slide 2-4: Same testimonials
- Slide 5: Same CTA
**Tests whether a stat-driven hook outperforms the social proof number (400+).**

### Variation C -- Longer Carousel (7 slides)
**Add 2 more testimonial slides:**
- Slide 5: "I'm a single mom working nights. This is the first thing that worked WITH my schedule, not against it." -- Rachel, RN
- Slide 6: "My rings fit again. That's when I knew something was actually changing." -- Kim, CNA, rotating shifts
- Slide 7: CTA slide
**Tests whether more proof = more conversion, or whether 5 slides is the sweet spot.**

### Variation D -- Alternate Hook Slide
**Slide 1 alternate text:** "Still think your body is broken? Read what these nurses found out."
**Tests whether a pain-hook opener outperforms the social proof opener.**
