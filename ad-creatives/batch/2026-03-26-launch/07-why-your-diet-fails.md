## THE HOOK (0-3 seconds)

**On-screen text:** "Why your diet fails after night shift -- an RN explains"

**IJ says:** "Let me explain why your diet keeps failing after night shift."

**Visual:** IJ speaking to camera. Calm, authoritative energy. Simple background. This is the "professor" ad -- he's teaching, not selling.

**Sound:** IJ's voice only. No music.

**IJ says:** "When you work a 12-hour shift -- especially nights -- your body produces cortisol for the entire shift. Cortisol is a stress hormone. In short bursts, it's fine."

**Visual:** Same talking-head. IJ is measured, clinical-but-accessible. Like explaining something to a patient.

**On-screen text:** "Cortisol = stress hormone"

## PRODUCTION NOTES

**Setting:** Simple indoor background. Bookshelf, clean wall, or home office. Somewhere that subtly says "smart" without trying.

**Wardrobe:** Scrubs or smart-casual. Scrubs reinforce the RN credential without stating it.

**Energy level:** Calm authority. This is a teaching moment, not a sales pitch. IJ is the colleague who read the study and is explaining it at the nurses' station. No hype. No urgency. The education IS the selling.

**Camera:** Phone on tripod. Vertical (9:16). Standard framing -- chest up, eyes at lens level.

**Editing notes:**
- Auto-captions throughout (critical for this educational format)
- Text overlays emphasize key mechanism points -- this is the "learn something" ad
- No music. Silence reinforces the authority tone.
- This ad runs LONGER than TOF ads (30-40 sec) because warm audiences will watch longer. Don't cut it short.
- The quiz CTA is intentionally lower commitment than "buy now" -- this is an MOF bridge ad.
