## PRODUCTION NOTES

**Design tool:** Canva or Figma.

**Sizes needed:**
- 1080x1080 (feed)
- 1080x1920 (Stories)
- 1200x628 (Facebook feed)

**Design principles:**
- The $27 is the centerpiece. It should be the first thing the eye hits.
- "Less than one DoorDash order" is the reframe that makes the price feel trivial. It should be immediately visible after the price.
- The detail block provides the rational justification -- what they get for $27.
- The guarantee removes the last barrier.
- This is a RETARGETING ad. The viewer already knows what SmithFit is. They need one final nudge. The price comparison is that nudge.
- Gold/cream aesthetic. Clean. No clutter.

**Why DoorDash specifically:** Nurses frequently order DoorDash/Uber Eats after long shifts. It's a shared experience for the avatar. The comparison lands because it's something she already spends $27+ on regularly without thinking twice.

## A/B VARIATIONS

### Variation A -- "Less Than a Starbucks Run" Reframe
**Replace DoorDash comparison with:**
"$27. Less than a week of Starbucks."
**Tests whether the daily-habit comparison (coffee) outperforms the post-shift comparison (DoorDash).**

### Variation B -- Split Comparison Layout
**Image layout:**
```
Left side:                    Right side:
One DoorDash order:          7-Day Metabolism Reset:
$30-$45                       $27
Gone in 20 minutes            Results by Day 3
Nothing changes               400+ nurses have done it
                              60-day guarantee
```
**Tests whether a visual comparison format drives more clicks than a single-statement format.**

### Variation C -- Dark Background
**Same content, reversed colors:**
- Background: Dark charcoal (#2D2D2D)
- $27 text: Gold
- Supporting text: Cream/white
**Tests dark mode contrast in the feed. Most fitness ad images are light -- this stands out.**

### Variation D -- "Skip One DoorDash" Headline
**Replace main text with:** "Skip one DoorDash order this week."
**Supporting text:** "Use that $27 on something that actually makes you feel better."
**More prescriptive. Tells her exactly what to do. Tests action-oriented framing.**
