## THE HOOK (0-2 seconds)

**On-screen text:** "But I work night shifts."

**IJ says:** "But I work night shifts."

**Visual:** IJ on camera. Quick-cut editing style -- each objection is a separate "clip" with a distinct text overlay. IJ mimics the objection voice (slightly higher pitch, uncertain tone) then responds in his own confident voice.

**Sound:** IJ's voice only. The quick cuts create rhythm and energy.

## PRODUCTION NOTES

**Setting:** Simple background. Same as other talking-head ads.

**Wardrobe:** Scrubs. The credential matters for authority when handling objections.

**Energy level:** This ad has a DIFFERENT rhythm than the others. It's fast, confident, almost playful. IJ should deliver the "mimicked" objections with a slightly different voice -- not mocking, but clearly quoting someone else. His responses are quick, direct, no hesitation. The confidence IS the answer.

**Camera:** Phone on tripod. Vertical (9:16).

**Filming tip:** Film each objection-response pair as a separate take. The editor will cut them together quickly. This makes it easier for IJ -- he doesn't need to deliver the whole thing in one take.

**Editing notes:**
- Quick cuts between each objection (no transitions, just hard cuts)
- Each objection gets its own text overlay that appears, then shifts to the response
- Text overlays should use a different color or style for the objection (white) vs. the response (gold or bold)
- Auto-captions on throughout
- The pacing should feel like a drumbeat -- boom, boom, boom, boom, CTA
- No music. The rhythm of the cuts IS the music.
- Keep it tight -- 20-24 seconds max. This ad should feel punchy, not drawn out.

## A/B VARIATIONS

### Variation A -- "Questions I Get" Hook
**Alternate hook (0-2 sec):** "Questions I get every single day from nurses:"
**On-screen text:** "Questions nurses ask me every day"
**Same objection-response pairs. Slightly softer framing -- "questions" vs. "objections."**

### Variation B -- 3 Objections Instead of 4
**Remove Objection 4 (menopause) to tighten to 15-18 seconds.**
**Tests whether shorter = better completion rate for this format.**

### Variation C -- Stitch Format
**Open with a "stitch" of a generic fitness creator giving bad advice (e.g., "meal prep Sunday is the key to weight loss"). IJ responds: "That advice was designed for people who work 9 to 5. Here's what actually works for shift workers."**
**Then flows into the objection-buster format.**
**Tests whether the "response" format earns more engagement than standalone.**
