## SECTION 1: VALUE EQUATION ANALYSIS

```
VALUE = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)
```

### Dream Outcome -- Score: 8/10

**What the page does well:** The headline nails the real enemy ("Fight-or-Flight") instead of leading with weight loss. The empathy copy in the problem section uses her exact internal language -- "coffee and vibes," "raccoon pantry raids," tight scrubs, exhaustion. The identity mirrors section (8 detailed before/after stories) lets her see herself in the outcome. The "She's Already Inside You" close is pure identity-level selling -- not "lose 20 lbs" but "become who you already are."

**What's missing:** The page leans heavily toward "belly fat" and "weight loss" in the hero and throughout. The REAL dream outcome for Shift Nurse Sarah is not a number on a scale. It is:

- Walking into her shift feeling steady, not chaotic
- Scrubs fitting without sucking in her stomach
- Not being controlled by food at 2am
- Having energy left for her family after a shift
- Feeling like HERSELF again

The hero section leads with "belly fat" which is the surface symptom. The deeper dream outcome -- identity restoration -- lives at the bottom of the page ("She's Already Inside You") when it should be woven throughout.

**What would make it a 10:**

Replace the subhead. Current:
> "The only 7-day metabolism reset built by a nurse, for nurses -- no macros, no punishing cardio, no perfect schedule required. Works on day shift, night shift, and the weeks where everything falls apart."

Suggested:
> "The 7-day protocol that helps nurses over 35 feel like themselves again -- steady energy, scrubs fitting right, cravings quiet, no overhauling your life. Built by a nurse who has lived your schedule."

This moves the dream outcome from "metabolism reset" (mechanism) to "feel like themselves again" (identity). The mechanism should support the dream, not replace it.

Add a line under the hero CTA:
> "By Day 3, most nurses say their energy stabilizes and their cravings get quieter. By Day 7, their scrubs start fitting different."

This paints the dream outcome in concrete, sensory terms (Hormozi: "Sell the vacation, not the plane flight").

### Perceived Likelihood of Achievement -- Score: 8.5/10

**What the page does well:** This is one of the strongest areas. The proof stack is deep:
- "400+ nurses" (social proof)
- "87% feel a difference by Day 3" (statistical proof)
- "5 lbs in Week 1" (measurable proof)
- IJ's credentials as an RN (authority proof)
- 8 detailed identity mirrors with specific numbers (story proof)
- Kristal's -31 lbs story with the emotional detail "my husband noticed before I did" (aspirational proof)
- The "437 nurses have started this month" counter (momentum proof)

The page makes her believe this works for nurses specifically, not just "people."

**What's missing:**

1. **No proof from someone who sounds exactly like her RIGHT NOW.** The testimonials are all from women who succeeded. There is no "I was exactly where you are" bridge testimonial -- someone who says "I was working nights, living on coffee, had tried everything, and honestly thought my body was broken. Then..." That bridge is what makes skeptical prospects think "she WAS me."

2. **No proof of the mechanism itself.** The page explains cortisol disruption well, but never shows proof that regulating cortisol specifically led to the results. A line like "When we tested nurses who followed the cortisol-first protocol vs. nurses who just changed their diet, the cortisol-first group lost 3x more belly fat in the same timeframe" would massively increase belief. Even if this exact stat does not exist, IJ could reference the science: "Research from [journal] shows that shift workers with chronically elevated cortisol store 40% more abdominal fat -- and that targeted stress regulation reverses this in weeks, not months."

3. **The "437 nurses have started this month" number is not anchored.** Is that a lot? Compared to what? Consider: "437 nurses started this month -- that is more than the last 3 months combined. Something is happening." This creates momentum proof.

**What would make it a 10:**

Add one "bridge testimonial" near the top of the page (before the problem section). Format:

> "I almost did not buy this. I had tried keto, IF, counting everything. I work nights, I have two kids, and I honestly thought my body was broken. But by Day 3 my energy was different. By Week 2 my scrubs were looser. It was the first thing that actually worked WITH my schedule instead of against it." -- [Name], Night Shift RN, 14 years

This single testimonial, placed early, does more for perceived likelihood than 6 testimonials placed later. It lets her see herself BEFORE the transformation.

### Time Delay -- Score: 9/10

**What the page does well:** Time delay is communicated exceptionally. "Feel the difference by Day 3" is repeated multiple times. "5 lbs in Week 1" sets a concrete early milestone. "7-Day Reset" is in the product name itself. The whole structure screams speed without making unbelievable promises.

**What's missing:**

The only gap is that the page does not make clear WHEN she will feel the first micro-win. "Day 3" is good. But what about Day 1? What about the first hour? Hormozi's principle: the faster the first perceived result, the higher the value.

**What would make it a 10:**

Add a "What happens when you start" timeline somewhere before the first CTA:

> **Hour 1:** You will know your exact Metabolism Stress Type and why everything you have tried before did not work.
> **Day 1:** You will have your first shift-proof meal template and the Metabolic Cocktail protocol ready.
> **Day 3:** Most nurses report steadier energy and quieter cravings. This is when you feel the shift.
> **Day 7:** Scrubs fitting different. Scale moving. A sense of "oh -- THIS is what was missing."

This collapses time delay to zero by giving her a win in the first hour (clarity is a win).

### Effort & Sacrifice -- Score: 8.5/10

**What the page does well:** The page hammers low effort throughout:
- "No macros"
- "No punishing cardio"
- "No perfect schedule required"
- Work Day / Off Day / Chaos Day templates
- The Old Way vs Shift-Proof Way comparison directly contrasts high-effort failed methods with low-effort SmithFit methods

The Chaos Day concept is brilliant -- it acknowledges that some days everything falls apart and says "we built for that." This is one of the most powerful effort-reducers on the page.

**What's missing:**

1. **The product breakdown (5 Pillars) makes it look like a LOT of content.** Five pillars, each with a dollar value, plus a download pack, plus 3 bonuses. That is 9 items. From a Value Equation perspective, more items = higher perceived effort ("I have to consume all of this?"). The stack adds value but also adds perceived work.

2. **No "done-for-you" language.** The page says what she does NOT have to do (no macros, no cardio) but does not emphasize what is done FOR her. "Pre-built templates" is mentioned but not emphasized.

**What would make it a 10:**

Add a line near the value stack:

> "You do not have to figure anything out. Every meal template, every protocol, every tool is built and waiting for you. Open it, follow it, feel the difference. That is it."

And add a parenthetical after the pillar stack:

> "(Total time commitment: 15-20 minutes per day. Most of that is eating the food we planned for you.)"

This directly counters the "this looks like a lot" objection created by a big value stack.

---

### Overall Value Equation Score: 8.5/10

The page is strong across all four variables. The biggest opportunities:
1. **Dream Outcome:** Lead with identity restoration, not belly fat (move from 8 to 10)
2. **Perceived Likelihood:** Add a bridge testimonial and mechanism proof (move from 8.5 to 10)
3. **Time Delay:** Add a Day 0/Hour 1 timeline (move from 9 to 10)
4. **Effort & Sacrifice:** Add done-for-you emphasis and time commitment clarity (move from 8.5 to 10)

## SECTION 2: GRAND SLAM OFFER AUDIT

### Core Offer: Is It Clear?

**Score: 7.5/10**

The 5 Pillars are listed with names and values, but the pillar names are abstract (Diagnose, Stabilize, Calm, Strengthen, Sustain). These are internal program architecture, not benefit language. She does not wake up wanting to "Diagnose" -- she wakes up wanting to know why nothing works. She does not want to "Stabilize" -- she wants to stop crashing at 2am.

**Fix:** Rename the pillars in the value stack to benefit-driven names:

| Current | Suggested |
|---------|-----------|
| Pillar 1: Diagnose ($75) | "Why You're Stuck" -- Your Metabolism Stress-Type Diagnosis ($75) |
| Pillar 2: Stabilize ($87) | "Stop the Crash" -- Your Shift-Proof Nutrition System ($87) |
| Pillar 3: Calm ($87) | "Calm the Alarm" -- The Nervous System Reset Protocol ($87) |
| Pillar 4: Strengthen ($107) | "Build Without Burning Out" -- 15-Min Cortisol-Conscious Workouts ($107) |
| Pillar 5: Sustain ($85) | "Never Start Over" -- Your Shift-Proof Maintenance System ($85) |

This applies Hormozi's principle: "Name every component with a benefit-driven name that could stand alone as a product."

### Bonuses: Do They Solve the Next Problem?

**Score: 7/10**

Current bonuses:
1. Grocery Guide & 15-Min Meal Prep ($47)
2. Beat the Binge: After-Shift Protocol ($47)
3. Restaurant & Takeout Survival Guide ($47)

**What's good:** These are practical, obstacle-specific tools. They address real problems (grocery shopping, after-shift binges, eating out).

**What's missing per Hormozi:** Bonuses should solve the NEXT problem after the core offer works. The core offer solves "why am I stuck and what do I do for 7 days." The next problems are:

- "What happens after Day 7?" (continuity gap)
- "What if I fall off?" (failure recovery)
- "How do I know it's working?" (measurement gap)

The current bonuses solve parallel problems (grocery shopping, eating out) rather than sequential problems. They are good but not strategically optimized.

**Suggested bonus restructure:**

| # | Bonus Name | Obstacle It Solves | Value |
|---|-----------|-------------------|-------|
| 1 | "Beat the Binge: After-Shift Protocol" (keep) | Post-shift sabotage | $47 |
| 2 | "The Day 8 Bridge Plan" (new) | "What do I do after the reset ends?" | $67 |
| 3 | "The Shift-Proof Grocery Blueprint" (rename) | Decision fatigue at the store | $47 |

The "Day 8 Bridge Plan" is the critical missing bonus. Right now, the offer creates a 7-day container with no visible bridge to what comes next. This bonus solves that AND creates a natural upsell path to the $67/mo membership ("Day 8 gives you the bridge -- the membership gives you the road").

### Guarantee: Strong Enough?

**Score: 8/10**

The 60-day money-back guarantee is solid for a $27 product. The "one-email refund" language reduces friction. The guarantee copy on the page is warm and non-corporate.

**What would make it a 10:**

Stack an unconditional + conditional guarantee (Hormozi's guarantee stacking strategy):

> "Try the full 7-Day Reset. If you do not love it for any reason, email us within 60 days for a full refund. No questions, no guilt, no forms.
>
> But here is what we really believe: if you follow the protocol for 7 days and do not feel a measurable difference in your energy and cravings, we do not just want to refund you -- we want to personally help you figure out what is going on. Email IJ directly and he will review your situation."

The conditional layer adds conviction (we are so confident we will personally troubleshoot for you) without adding risk (the unconditional guarantee is still there).

### Scarcity: Is It Real?

**Score: 3/10**

There is no scarcity on the page. The "437 nurses have started this month" is social proof, not scarcity. The product is a digital course available indefinitely.

**The problem:** Without scarcity, there is no cost to waiting. And the #1 competitor for any $27 offer is not another product -- it is "I will do it later."

**What would be ethical and real:**

Option A -- Bonus scarcity:
> "The 3 Fast-Action Bonuses ($141 value) are included free this week. After [date], they move to a separate $47 add-on."

This is real if you actually do it. Digital bonuses can genuinely be restructured.

Option B -- Price scarcity:
> "The $27 price is our launch price. It will increase to $47 as we add more tools and content. Lock in $27 now."

This is real if IJ intends to raise the price (and he should, per Hormozi's virtuous cycle).

### Urgency: What Creates a Reason to Act NOW?

**Score: 4/10**

The only urgency on the page is the emotional urgency of the future-pacing close ("Future A vs Future B"). That is good copywriting but it is not structural urgency.

**What to add:**

> "Every week you wait, your body spends another 168 hours in fight-or-flight mode. That is another week of cortisol storing fat, disrupting your sleep, and making cravings louder. The protocol takes 7 days. You could be on Day 3 by [dynamic: day of week + 3 days]."

This creates temporal urgency tied to the mechanism, not fake countdown timers.

### Naming: MAGIC Formula Test

**"7-Day Shift-Proof Metabolism Reset"**

| Element | Present? | Assessment |
|---------|----------|------------|
| **M** (Magnetic reason) | Partial | "Shift-Proof" implies the reason but does not state it explicitly |
| **A** (Avatar) | Missing | Does not say "for nurses" in the name |
| **G** (Goal) | Yes | "Metabolism Reset" = the goal |
| **I** (Interval) | Yes | "7-Day" |
| **C** (Container) | Yes | "Reset" |

**Score: 3.5/5 MAGIC elements present**

The name is already strong. To make it a 5/5:

> "The 7-Day Shift-Proof Metabolism Reset for Nurses Over 35"

Or in shorter form for ads:

> "The Nurse's 7-Day Metabolism Reset"

The word "Nurse" in the name is the single highest-impact naming change. When she sees "for nurses" she immediately self-selects: "this is FOR ME." Every other metabolism reset on Instagram is generic. This one has her name on it.

## SECTION 3: CONVICTION ANALYSIS

### Does IJ Come Across as Someone Who BELIEVES?

**Score: 8.5/10**

IJ's origin story is one of the strongest sections on the page. The travel nurse background, the "tried everything wrong" admission, the mirror moment -- this is textbook conviction selling. He is not pitching from a stage. He is sharing what he learned the hard way. The conviction is palpable because:

1. He is a nurse talking to nurses (shared identity)
2. He admits he got it wrong first (damaging admission builds trust)
3. He built this for a specific person, not everyone (specificity = conviction)
4. The "400+ nurses" stat backs up his belief with evidence

### Where Is Conviction Strongest?

- **IJ's origin story** -- This is the emotional peak of the page. The mirror moment, the admission of failure, the pivot to creating the protocol. This reads like a person talking, not a marketer selling.
- **The testimonials** -- Kristal's story ("my husband noticed before I did") has real emotion. The identity mirrors section feels like IJ collected these stories personally.
- **The problem section** -- "Coffee and vibes" and "raccoon pantry raids" are the kind of specific, funny, warm details that only come from someone who has actually listened to hundreds of nurses describe their lives.

### Where Does Conviction Weaken?

1. **The value stack section.** When the page shifts to listing pillars with dollar values ($75, $87, $87, $107, $85), it suddenly sounds like a Clickfunnels template. The warm nurse-to-nurse voice disappears and "marketer math" takes over. IJ would never say to a nurse in the break room, "This is worth $679." He would say, "I put everything I know into this. It is $27. If it does not work, I will give you your money back."

   **Fix:** Keep the value stack (it works for the conscious mind) but add IJ's voice around it:
   > "Here is everything inside. I could price each piece separately and charge $679 for the stack. But I remember being the nurse who could not afford another thing that might not work. So it is $27. All of it. And if it does not help, you get every penny back."

2. **The FAQ section.** FAQs are often where conviction dies because they shift into defensive, corporate-sounding language. The current FAQ is decent but could be warmer.

   **Fix for FAQ #1 ("Will this work if I work nights?"):**
   Current: Unknown (not provided in full)
   Suggested: "This was built ON night shift. IJ is a travel nurse. His wife coaches. Most of our members work nights. We have Work Day templates, Off Day templates, and Chaos Day templates because your schedule is not 9-to-5, and we have never pretended it is."

3. **The pricing anchor.** The "$679 value / $27 today / 96% off / $652 savings" math feels like an infomercial. Hormozi's actual advice on this: the value stack builds perceived value, but the discount framing can cheapen it. "96% off" makes it sound like a clearance sale, not a premium offer at an accessible price.

   **Fix:** Remove "96% off" and "$652 savings." Replace with:
   > "Total value if purchased separately: $679. Your price today: $27. One payment. Lifetime access."

   This communicates the same gap without the discount-store framing.

## SECTION 4: OBJECTION HANDLING AUDIT

### Objection 1: "Works on nights?"
**Addressed?** Yes -- strongly.
**Where:** Night Shift Section, Work Day/Off Day/Chaos Day templates, hero subhead ("Works on day shift, night shift..."), FAQ
**How well:** 9/10 -- This is one of the page's biggest strengths. The entire page is structured around shift work, not adapted to it.
**Gap:** Could be even stronger if the hero included "night shift" more prominently. "Works on day shift, night shift" is buried in the subhead.

### Objection 2: "Do I track macros?"
**Addressed?** Yes.
**Where:** Hero subhead ("no macros"), Old Way vs Shift-Proof Way section
**How well:** 8/10 -- Clear and direct.
**Gap:** The phrase "no macros" appears but the alternative is not explicitly stated nearby. Add: "No macros. No calorie counting. You follow shift-proof templates instead -- Work Day, Off Day, Chaos Day. Structure beats tracking."

### Objection 3: "What if I miss breaks?"
**Addressed?** Partially.
**Where:** Implied in "no perfect schedule required" and Chaos Day templates
**How well:** 5/10 -- Never directly addressed.
**Fix:** Add to the problem section or FAQ: "What if you miss breaks? Good. We built for that. The Chaos Day protocol is specifically designed for the shifts where nothing goes according to plan -- because that is most of them."

### Objection 4: "I've tried everything."
**Addressed?** Yes -- strongly.
**Where:** Hero headline ("tried everything"), Problem section, IJ's origin story, FAQ
**How well:** 9/10 -- The reframe from "you tried effort" to "you have not tried regulation" is excellent. The mechanism explanation (cortisol, not discipline) directly answers this objection.
**Gap:** Could be strengthened by adding one line in the hero area: "You have tried everything that works for people with normal schedules. This is the first thing built for YOUR schedule."

### Objection 5: "Menopause/perimenopause -- nothing works."
**Addressed?** Partially.
**Where:** FAQ section
**How well:** 5/10 -- Mentioned but not given the weight it deserves. For nurses 35-52, peri/menopause is a MAJOR concern. It deserves more than a FAQ answer.
**Fix:** Add a dedicated callout box before the pricing section:
> "Going through perimenopause or menopause? Hormonal changes make your body MORE sensitive to cortisol -- which is exactly why this approach works BETTER during this stage, not worse. Several of our strongest transformations are women in their late 40s who had tried everything else. Your hormones are not broken. Your stress response needs regulation first."

### Objection 6: "Too exhausted to work out."
**Addressed?** Yes.
**Where:** "No punishing cardio" in hero, Old Way vs Shift-Proof Way
**How well:** 7/10 -- The "no punishing cardio" line is clear, but it does not address what she DOES do instead.
**Fix:** Add near the workout pillar: "The workouts are 15 minutes. They are designed to send your body a signal, not punish it. And on the days you are too tired for even that? We have a 5-minute backup. Because something always beats nothing."

### Objection 7: "Just a meal plan?"
**Addressed?** Partially.
**Where:** The 5-pillar breakdown shows it is more than a meal plan
**How well:** 6/10 -- The value stack implies depth, but no copy directly reframes this.
**Fix:** Add to the product intro: "This is not a meal plan. It is a shift-proof physiology system -- starting with WHY your body is stuck, then giving you the nutrition, nervous system, movement, and sustainability tools to actually fix it."

### Objection 8: "I can't afford this."
**Addressed?** Partially.
**Where:** $27 price point itself, 60-day guarantee
**How well:** 6/10 -- The price is low enough that the objection is mild, but it is never directly reframed.
**Fix:** Add near pricing: "You have probably spent more than $27 this month on supplements, coffees, or takeout meals that did not move the needle. This is one payment, lifetime access, and a 60-day guarantee. If it does not work, you get it back."

### Objection 9: "What if I fail again?"
**Addressed?** Partially.
**Where:** Problem section validates her past failures, guarantee reduces risk
**How well:** 5/10 -- The page validates that past approaches failed but does not directly address the fear of THIS failing.
**Fix:** Add before the final CTA: "If you are worried about failing again -- that fear makes sense. You have started things before. They did not stick. But here is what was different about those: they required you to fight your body's stress response. This works WITH it. The reason 87% feel different by Day 3 is because for the first time, they stopped fighting and started regulating."

### Objection 10: "Is this a scam?"
**Addressed?** Partially.
**Where:** IJ's credentials (RN), origin story, guarantee, testimonials
**How well:** 7/10 -- The RN credential and personal story build trust. The testimonials with specific results build credibility. The 60-day guarantee reduces risk.
**Gap:** No explicit "here is why this is different from the garbage you have seen on Instagram" statement. The page assumes trust. For cold traffic from ads, add: "Built by IJ Smith, RN -- not an influencer, not a fitness model, not someone selling you their genetics. A nurse who figured this out the hard way and has helped 400+ nurses do the same."

## SECTION 5: QUIZ AUDIT

### Does the quiz create enough curiosity to complete?

**Score: 7/10**

**What works:**
- The headline "What's Really Keeping You Stuck?" creates genuine curiosity
- The 2-minute time commitment is low-friction
- The avatar callout ("Built specifically for nurses and healthcare workers over 35") immediately qualifies the right person
- The 3-type structure (Under-Fueled & Stressed / Sleep-Deprived & Overstimulated / Blood-Sugar Rollercoaster) creates real curiosity about which type she is

**What's missing:**
- There is no stakes framing on the intro screen. She knows she will find her "type" but does not know WHY it matters. Add: "Your type determines the exact protocol that works for your body. Get the wrong approach and you stay stuck. Get the right one and most nurses feel different by Day 3."
- The quiz shows all 15 questions at once across 3 categories, which reveals the types before she finishes. This reduces the "reveal" moment. Consider hiding the type labels (A, B, C) during the quiz or at minimum removing the type names from the section headers during the quiz phase. Let her check boxes without knowing what they map to, then reveal the type.

### Does the email gate provide enough value to justify the email?

**Score: 6/10**

Current copy:
> "See Your Results -- Get your personalized type + action plan. We'll send your full results and a special message from IJ."

**Problems:**
1. "See Your Results" is generic. She has already done the work. The value exchange needs to feel bigger.
2. "A special message from IJ" is vague and low-value.
3. There is no reason to believe the emailed results will be better than what she sees on screen.

**Fix:**

> "Your Type is Ready"
>
> "See your full Metabolism Stress-Type breakdown, including your #1 priority action, the trap that keeps your type stuck, and what to do on your worst day. Plus: IJ recorded a 2-minute voice note for your specific type."
>
> [First name] [Email]
>
> "Show Me My Type"

This works because:
- "Your Type is Ready" implies the result exists and is waiting (Hormozi: perceived effort already invested)
- The specifics (priority action, trap, worst-day protocol) make the results feel genuinely valuable
- "2-minute voice note for your specific type" is a high-value, low-effort bonus that justifies the email
- "Show Me My Type" is more personal than "Get My Results"

### Does the results page create enough desire for the $27 offer?

**Score: 6.5/10**

**What works:**
- The results are genuinely useful. Each type gets a #1 priority, a comparison (what most nurses do vs. what works), focus cues, a trap to stop, a chaos-day minimum, and proof-by-day markers. This is real value.
- The "What's Happening In Your Body" section gives her the mechanism explanation she has been craving.
- Trust signals (400+, 87%, ~5 lbs, 60-Day) are present.

**What's missing (per Hormozi's Pretzel Principle):**

The results solve the DIAGNOSIS but do not create enough THIRST for the implementation. The Pretzel Principle says: solve one narrow problem completely, and that solution should reveal a bigger problem that your paid offer solves.

Right now, the results tell her what type she is AND what to do about it. That is too complete. She should leave the quiz thinking "I know WHAT is wrong and WHAT the solution category is, but I need the actual system to implement it."

**Fix the results page structure:**

1. **Keep:** Type diagnosis, "What's Happening In Your Body," the trap, the proof-by-day markers
2. **Reduce:** The "Focus Cues" section gives away too much implementation. Change from 4 specific cues to 2 teaser cues + "The full protocol for your type is inside the 7-Day Reset"
3. **Add a gap statement** between results and CTA:

> "Now you know your type. You know why you have been stuck. But knowing your type is like knowing you have a flat tire -- it does not fix the tire. The 7-Day Reset gives you the exact protocol for YOUR type: the meals, the timing, the nervous system tools, the chaos-day backups. Everything built for [Type A/B/C] nurses on shifts."

This creates the "thirst" the Pretzel Principle requires.

### Is the CTA section strong enough?

**Score: 5.5/10**

Current CTA headline:
> "Ready to Actually Fix This?"

Current value stack lists 6 items generically. The CTA button says "Start Your Reset -- $27."

**Problems:**
1. "Ready to Actually Fix This?" is a generic CTA headline used on thousands of landing pages.
2. The value stack items do not connect to HER TYPE. She just learned she is Type A (Under-Fueled & Stressed), but the CTA sells a generic product.

**Fix the entire CTA section:**

> - Shift-proof templates: Work Day, Off Day, and Chaos Day
> - The Metabolic Cocktail that resets your morning cortisol
> - The Nervous System Reset tool (5 minutes, built for post-shift)
> - The interactive tracker so you can see the shift happening
> - 60-day guarantee -- if you do not feel different, you get every penny back
>
> **Button:** "Start My Type [A] Reset -- $27"
>
> **Under button:** "One-time payment. Lifetime access. Built by IJ Smith, RN."

This applies Hormozi's conviction selling principle: the offer should feel like it was MADE for her, not adapted to her.

### What's missing based on Hormozi's lead magnet framework?

1. **The quiz does not send the type to GHL with the webhook.** The webhook sends firstName, email, and timestamp -- but not the quiz result type. GHL should receive the type so the follow-up email sequence can be personalized. This is critical for the nurture sequence.

Send her the quiz -- it takes 2 minutes and it might change how she thinks about her body." Social sharing is Hormozi's "customers as lead generators" from $100M Leads.
