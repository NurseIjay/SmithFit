4. Critical & High-Priority Issues

🔴 CRITICAL — Conversion Killer
Male Bodybuilder Photo on a Female Nurse Page
A shredded male before/after photo appears near the bottom of the page. Your avatar is a 35-52 year old female nurse. This single image may be tanking your conversion rate by 30-50% for anyone who scrolls to it. It signals "this isn't for people like me" and contradicts every word of copy above it.
Fix: Remove immediately. Replace with a female nurse transformation (or remove the section entirely until you have one). Even a testimonial screenshot from your community would be better.
🔴 CRITICAL — Brand Confusion
"Hayley Smith, RN" — Who Is This?
The personal note section is signed "Hayley Smith, RN" but your brand is built around IJ Smith, RN. If Hayley is your wife, she needs to be introduced as such. A prospect who just bought from IJ now sees a stranger's name — this creates confusion and breaks the parasocial trust built through your content.
Fix: Either change to IJ's name and voice, or introduce Hayley properly: "A Note from Hayley Smith, RN — IJ's wife and your Head Coach inside the Sisterhood."
🔴 CRITICAL — Visibility
Page-Wide Text Contrast Failure
At least 8 sections have text so light it's nearly invisible against the cream/beige background. Testimonial cards, feature descriptions, pricing details, and the "Imagine This" section all suffer. If prospects can't READ your copy, it doesn't matter how good it is. This is likely even worse on mobile.
Fix: Audit every text element. Body text should be at minimum #333333 on light backgrounds. Headlines should be #1a1a1a or darker. Run the entire page through a WCAG contrast checker.
🔴 CRITICAL — Trust
Inconsistent Social Proof Numbers
The page alternates between "2,847+" and "283+" nurses. Your CLAUDE.md says "400+ nurses transformed." Three different numbers = zero credibility. Any savvy buyer will notice this.
Fix: Pick ONE real number and use it everywhere. If it's 400+, use 400+. Round up slightly if needed (e.g., "400+" is fine if you have 387). Never inflate by 7x.
🟠 HIGH — Revenue Impact
$349 "Regular Price" Feels Fabricated
Claiming a "regular price" of $349/mo crossed out to $67/mo (81% off) triggers skepticism in educated buyers. Nurses are smart. If this was never actually $349, you're trading short-term urgency for long-term trust damage. Hormozi's principle: use REAL scarcity and pricing, not manufactured discounts.
Fix: Either (a) justify the $349 by showing what comparable programs charge, or (b) reframe as "launch price" or "charter member rate" instead of a fake crossed-out price. "Join during our launch for just $67/month — this price locks in for life" is more honest and still urgent.
🟠 HIGH — Lost Revenue
Features Not Stacked as Hormozi Bonuses
The 6 features (coaching, recipes, workouts, community, chaos plans, monthly resets) are listed as "what you get." In Hormozi's framework, each should be a NAMED BONUS with its own perceived dollar value, building a value stack that dwarfs the price. Currently the value anchor is one line: "Total value: $1,300+/month" — but no individual values are broken out.
Fix: Reframe each feature as a bonus with an individual value. Example: "BONUS 1: 1,000+ Recipe Library ($97/mo value) — Your favorite comfort food, shift-proofed." Stack all values visually, then show the total vs. the $67 price. This is Hormozi 101.
🟠 HIGH — Mobile
Massive Whitespace + Broken Layout at Top
The page opens with a huge blank white area before any content. On desktop this means ~2 full scrolls of nothing. On mobile this will be even worse — users will bounce thinking the page didn't load.
Fix: Remove all excess padding/margin at the top. The confirmation text + headline should be immediately visible on page load without scrolling.
🟠 HIGH — Urgency
Countdown Timer Not Rendering
"THIS PRICE EXPIRES IN" appears twice but no actual countdown numbers are visible. A deadline without a visible countdown is just text — it creates no urgency. Combined with the "81% off today only" claim, this makes the urgency feel hollow.
Fix: Either fix the countdown timer to actually display, or remove the urgency claim entirely. A broken timer is worse than no timer.
🟡 MEDIUM
Video Thumbnail Is a Waterfall (Not a Nurse)
The VSL thumbnail shows a nature/waterfall scene. In direct response, the thumbnail IS the ad — it determines whether people click play. A waterfall has zero connection to nursing, metabolism, or the avatar's pain.
Fix: Custom thumbnail of IJ speaking to camera, or a nurse in scrubs, or a text overlay that says "What Happens After Day 7? (Watch This)" with a play button.
🟡 MEDIUM
Testimonials Are Text-Only — No Visual Proof
All 4 testimonials are text with names and roles. No photos, no DM screenshots, no video. In 2026, text-only testimonials on a sales page are widely assumed to be AI-generated or fabricated.
Fix: Add at least: headshot photos next to each testimonial, or screenshots of real DM conversations, or short video clips. The Crystal M. full-width photo section is the model — do more of that.
🟡 MEDIUM
Guarantee Buried Too Deep
The "Nurse's Honor 30-Day Guarantee" doesn't appear until section 16 of ~20. Hormozi teaches that the guarantee should appear right after the price reveal to immediately neutralize the "what if it doesn't work" objection at the moment of highest buyer anxiety.
Fix: Add a mini guarantee badge or one-liner directly under the first pricing CTA, then keep the full guarantee section where it is for those who scroll.
