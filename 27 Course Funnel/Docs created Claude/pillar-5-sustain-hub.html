If-This-Then-That Scenario Library

15 real situations. Real responses. Bookmark the ones you need.

Nutrition Scenarios
I missed breakfast ▼
Then:
Protein-first at first opportunity
  • Protein shake + fruit, or eggs + toast, or Greek yogurt + granola
  • Eat this within the hour of waking
  • Don't try to "catch up" with larger meals — just reset the anchor
  • Tomorrow, set a phone alarm for breakfast
Why: Breakfast is your metabolic ignition. Missed or skipped = your body stays in conservation mode all day. Hitting protein early, even if "late," restarts the signal.
I missed breaks — couldn't eat ▼
Then:
Mini-fuel + hydration at first chance
  • Protein shake, bar, or Greek yogurt the moment you can
  • Drink a full bottle of water + electrolytes or salt
  • Don't panic. One skipped break isn't a reset failure
  • Your next structured meal is your comeback
Why: Missed breaks = missed cortisol resets. You can't control your schedule, but you can control the first refuel. Protein + hydration tells your body you're not in emergency mode.
Cravings hit mid-shift ▼
Then:
Protein + fiber + 2-min breathing
  • String cheese + apple, or protein bar + water, or handful of almonds + fruit
  • Do a 2-minute box breathing or SOS reset
  • The craving usually passes in 10 minutes
  • Bonus: You just protected your momentum without willpower
Why: Mid-shift cravings are cortisol spikes, not hunger. Protein + fiber + breathing = regulated nervous system = the craving dissolves.
I overate at dinner ▼
Then:
No punishment. Walk + normal anchor tomorrow.
  • Do NOT restrict calories or skip meals tomorrow
  • Do NOT do a "punishment" workout
  • Take a 10–15 min walk after dinner if you can (digestion + breathing)
  • Tomorrow: Protein anchor + normal meals. That's it.
Why: One meal doesn't derail your metabolism. Restriction and shame do. Your body is resilient. Consistency matters more than perfection.
I didn't drink enough water ▼
Then:
"2 bottle rule" from now until bed
  • Drink 2 full bottles of water for every cup of coffee
  • Add salt to one bottle (electrolytes matter)
  • This rehydrates and resets your craving signals
  • You'll likely sleep better and feel less hungry tomorrow
Why: Dehydration feels like hunger. It's the #1 reason nurses reach for snacks at 2 AM. Fix hydration, fix cravings.
Sleep & Recovery Scenarios
I slept less than 5 hours ▼
Then:
Yellow day: anchors only, no HIIT, 10-min walk or 5-min reset
  • Your cortisol is already high. Don't add intensity.
  • Protein anchor + hydration + 2 structured meals
  • One easy workout (10-min walk or yoga), or skip it
  • Do 2 resets today: one in the morning, one before bed
  • Prioritize sleep tomorrow — it's your #1 tool
Why: Poor sleep = poor nervous system regulation = your body is in fight-or-flight. HIIT will just amp you up. Anchor + easy movement = safe reset.
I'm wired after my shift ▼
Then:
2-min car reset + lights down + phone face-down
  • Do the 2-minute car reset before leaving work if possible
  • Dim the lights when you get home (lower cortisol)
  • Put your phone face-down for 30 mins
  • Eat a light protein meal (not heavy carbs — you need to downshift, not fuel up)
  • Cool shower + gentle stretching 2 hours before bed
Why: Your nervous system is flooded from the shift. You need to downshift, not sleep. Resets + darkness + no screens = your body gets the "shift over" signal.
My shift flipped (days ↔ nights) ▼
Then:
Anchor meal after waking, set caffeine boundary, 5-min reset before sleep
  • Whatever time you wake (day or night), eat protein first
  • Caffeine ONLY in the first 4 hours after waking
  • Eat your largest meal in the middle of your "day" (whenever that is)
  • Do a 5-min reset 2 hours before your sleep window
  • Give yourself 3-4 days to fully adjust. You're not failing — you're adapting.
Why: Flip days mess with your circadian rhythm. The anchor meal resets your internal clock. Caffeine timing prevents wakefulness when you want to sleep.
Movement & Consistency Scenarios
I missed my workout ▼
Then:
Do the 10-min version tomorrow (1 circuit or 10-min walk). Never miss twice.
  • Missing one workout = life happens. It's fine.
  • Missing two in a row = habit break. That's the pattern you want to avoid.
  • Tomorrow, do ANY movement (1 circuit, 10-min walk, yoga) to restart the streak
  • You're building consistency, not perfection
Why: Habits thrive on consistency, not intensity. A 10-minute easy walk beats missing back-to-back days. The goal is the signal, not the sweat.
I feel puffy or inflamed ▼
Then:
Stabilize, don't restrict. Protein + hydration + walk.
  • Do NOT cut calories or restrict food
  • DO eat MORE protein (helps reduce inflammation)
  • DO drink more water + electrolytes (counterintuitive but it helps)
  • DO take a 15–20 min walk (gentle movement reduces inflammation)
  • This usually passes in 24–48 hours. You're not gaining fat.
Why: Puffiness is usually inflammation, not fat gain. Restriction makes it worse. Stabilization (protein + water + movement) is what actually works.
Mindset & Motivation Scenarios
I feel like starting over ▼
Then:
Don't. Pick your day color (Green/Yellow/Red). Do the minimum. Tomorrow is a new check-in.
  • "Starting over" is perfectionism. You haven't failed — you just had a chaotic day.
  • Check your day color: Are you green, yellow, or red today?
  • Do only what that color requires. That's your win.
  • Tomorrow you reassess. There's no "restart." Just the next day.
Why: The "restart" mindset keeps you stuck in the all-or-nothing cycle. Reality is fluid. Progress is messy. Keep moving.
I had a terrible week ▼
Then:
Restart with Chaos Day template tomorrow. Just 2 checkmarks. Build from there.
  • Don't spiral. Bad weeks happen. That's normal.
  • Pick the two easiest non-negotiables (e.g., protein anchor + hydration)
  • Do ONLY those two things for 3 days
  • Then reassess. You'll likely move to yellow day
  • Momentum rebuilds in small steps, not giant leaps
Why: Low expectations reset your nervous system and rebuild trust in yourself. You're not broken — you just needed to simplify.
I'm not seeing scale changes ▼
Then:
Check your non-scale wins: energy, cravings, sleep, bloat, clothes fit. The scale lags behind stability changes.
  • Your body is changing before the scale moves (this is real)
  • Ask: Do my clothes fit better? Am I less bloated? Do I have more energy?
  • Those changes = your metabolism is working
  • Scale changes follow in 3–6 weeks, not 3–6 days
  • If you're seeing non-scale wins, you're on track. Keep going.
Why: The scale doesn't measure metabolic repair. Stability, energy, and body composition changes come before weight loss. You're progressing even if the scale is quiet.
Life Disruptions Scenarios
Holiday / vacation / travel ▼
Then:
Protein anchor is your only non-negotiable. Everything else is optional. Enjoy your time.
  • One meal a day with a solid protein anchor keeps your body in regulation mode
  • Skip the guilt. Vacations are for living, not for control
  • Resets, workouts, structured meals? Optional. Do them if it feels good.
  • You're not "messing up." You're just pausing
  • When you get home, anchor + normal routine for 2–3 days to recalibrate
Why: Your metabolism doesn't reset on vacation. One anchor meal a day keeps your nervous system regulated while you enjoy freedom. True sustainability means enjoying life without obsessing.
Sick / family emergency ▼
Then:
Full pause is OK. When you're ready, start with one Red Day. Then Yellow. Then Green. No restart required.
  • You don't have to "stay on track" during a crisis. Life happens.
  • When you're ready to re-engage (2 days, 2 weeks, whatever), pick one Red Day
  • Red day = just one anchor (protein or reset). That's it.
  • When that feels easy, move to yellow (anchors + one easy walk)
  • From there, back to green. No guilt, no "starting over."
Why: Real life includes sickness, grief, and emergencies. Your body is resilient. Restarting isn't about punishment — it's about gradual recalibration. You're still moving forward.
