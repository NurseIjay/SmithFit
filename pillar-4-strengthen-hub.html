The 5-Minute Sequence

  1. 60 sec: Slow squats — 8–10 full-depth squats, 5 sec per rep. Activates quads, glutes, stabilizers. No impact.
  2. 60 sec: Wall push-ups — 12–15 controlled reps. Chest, shoulders, triceps. Adjustable intensity.
  3. 60 sec: Standing marches — High knees, steady pace. Elevate heart rate gently. Core engagement.
  4. 60 sec: Glute bridges (floor or chair) — 10–12 reps, 2 sec hold at top. Activate posterior chain.
  5. 60 sec: Cool-down breathing — Box breathing: 4 in, 4 hold, 4 out, 4 hold. Parasympathetic activation.

Why This Works on Tired Days

  • No prep: No equipment. Anywhere. Anytime.
  • Low impact: Protects nervous system, respects exhaustion.
  • Strength signal: Engages all major muscle groups in 5 minutes.
  • Parasympathetic finish: Breathing brings you back down, not up.
  • Momentum builder: Proves you can move, even on hard days.

Your 4-Phase Progress Roadmap

Phase 1: Consistency (Days 1–14)

Goal: Show up 5 days, any level. Prove to yourself movement is possible, even on hard days.

Win: Logged 5 sessions. Your nervous system recognizes "this is safe."

Phase 2: Intensity Awareness (Days 15–30)

Goal: Notice how your body responds. Are you choosing the right level? How does energy before/after compare?

Win: You see patterns (e.g., morning workouts = higher energy after than evening ones). Adjust accordingly.

Phase 3: Sustainable Rhythm (Days 31–60)

Goal: Find your "non-negotiable" minimum. Even if it's just 1×/week, consistency beats perfection.

Win: You have a real movement identity. "I'm someone who moves" even on shift work.

Phase 4: Progressive Adaptation (Days 61+)

Goal: Slowly add complexity or volume without disrupting life. Your foundation is solid.

Win: Body composition shifts. Clothes fit. Metabolism stabilized. Confidence rebuilt.
